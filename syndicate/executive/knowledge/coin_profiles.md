## TIER 1: MEGA CAPS (Market Cap >$100B)

### BTC — Bitcoin
- **Sector**: Store of Value / Digital Gold | **Launched**: 2009
- **Supply**: 21M hard cap. ~19.8M circulating (94.3%). ~3.125 BTC minted per block (post-April 2024 halving). Next halving ~March 2028.
- **ATH**: $126,296 (Oct 2025) | **Biggest drawdowns**: -87% (2014), -84% (2018), -78% (2022), -47% (current from ATH)
- **Price drivers**: (1) Fed rate policy / global liquidity (2) ETF inflows/outflows — BlackRock IBIT is the largest holder (3) Halving supply shock narrative (4) BTC dominance cycle (5) Risk-on/risk-off macro
- **Personality**: THE market leader. Moves first in every cycle. When BTC dumps, everything dumps harder. When BTC consolidates, alts can run. BTC dominance rises in fear, falls in greed.
- **Key risks**: Regulatory (US classification), quantum computing FUD (long-term), miner centralization, ETF outflow cascades
- **CEO notes**: BTC is always position #1. In extreme fear (F&G <15), historically the best buy signal in crypto. Never short BTC in a halving year. BTC above its 200 SMA with rising network power = bull market confirmed.

### ETH — Ethereum
- **Sector**: Smart Contract Platform / L1 | **Launched**: 2015
- **Supply**: No hard cap. Post-merge (Sept 2022) deflationary via EIP-1559 burn. ~120.5M circulating. ~27% staked. Net issuance near zero or slightly negative when gas is high.
- **ATH**: $4,815 (Nov 2021) | **Current**: ~70% below ATH — deep bear territory relative to BTC
- **Price drivers**: (1) DeFi TVL growth (2) L2 adoption (Arbitrum, Base, Optimism) (3) ETH/BTC ratio cycle (4) Gas fee revenue / burn rate (5) Spot ETH ETF flows (6) Staking yield
- **Personality**: Follows BTC with higher beta (1.2-1.5x). Leads alt season — when ETH/BTC ratio starts rising, alts follow within weeks. Underperforms BTC in early bull, outperforms in mid-to-late bull.
- **Key risks**: L2 value extraction (fees going to L2s not L1), SOL competition narrative, regulatory risk (SEC staking concerns), ETH/BTC ratio structural decline
- **CEO notes**: ETH/BTC below 0.03 = historically extreme undervaluation. ETH above 0.058 BTC = alt season signal. Watch DeFi TVL on Ethereum specifically. ETH is the gateway to the alt market — if ETH isn't working, alts won't work.

### BNB — Binance Coin
- **Sector**: Exchange Token / L1 | **Launched**: 2017
- **Supply**: Started at 200M. Quarterly burns targeting 100M final supply. ~145M circulating.
- **ATH**: $793 (Dec 2024)
- **Price drivers**: (1) Binance exchange dominance (2) BNB Chain TVL/activity (3) Launchpad/Launchpool demand (4) Quarterly burn events (5) Regulatory risk to Binance
- **Personality**: Most stable large-cap. Less volatile than BTC. Acts as a "safe haven" within crypto during crashes (relative outperformance). Launchpad events create temporary demand spikes.
- **Key risks**: Binance regulatory risk (DOJ settlement), CZ's legal situation, concentration risk (Binance controls everything)
- **CEO notes**: BNB is a defensive hold. It dumps less than everything else in crashes. Not exciting in bull markets but protects in bears. Watch for Binance regulatory news as binary risk.

### XRP — Ripple
- **Sector**: Payments / Cross-border | **Launched**: 2012
- **Supply**: 100B total. ~57B circulating. Ripple holds ~40B in escrow (releases monthly).
- **ATH**: $3.84 (Jan 2018)
- **Price drivers**: (1) SEC lawsuit resolution (largely resolved 2024-2025) (2) Banking/institutional partnerships (3) Escrow release schedule (4) Korea/Japan retail demand (5) XRP Ledger ecosystem growth
- **Personality**: Trades on its own cycle. Can be dormant for months then pump 50%+ in days. High retail following, especially in Asia. Pumps are often news-driven, not technical. Tends to pump LATE in bull cycles.
- **Key risks**: Escrow dilution (monthly unlocks), regulatory uncertainty in other jurisdictions, competition from stablecoins for payments
- **CEO notes**: XRP is a "narrative trade" — it pumps on partnership announcements and legal wins. Not a hold for steady returns. Best entered on clear catalyst. The escrow is a constant overhang.

### SOL — Solana
- **Sector**: Smart Contract Platform / L1 | **Launched**: 2020
- **Supply**: No hard cap. Inflationary (~5.4% declining). ~65% staked. ~430M circulating.
- **ATH**: $258 (Nov 2021) | **Crashed to $8 during FTX** (96.9% drawdown — and fully recovered)
- **Price drivers**: (1) Ecosystem growth (DeFi, NFTs, meme coins) (2) Transaction throughput / low fees (3) Firedancer validator client (4) Institutional adoption (5) Meme coin activity on Solana
- **Personality**: High beta to BTC (2-3x). Moves fast in both directions. The "momentum L1" — when crypto is hot, SOL is hotter. Meme coin activity on Solana is a leading indicator for SOL price.
- **Key risks**: Network outages (history of multiple), inflationary tokenomics, validator centralization, meme coin reputation risk
- **CEO notes**: SOL is the best momentum play in L1s. Its recovery from $8 to $250+ after FTX proves resilience. Watch Solana DEX volume and meme coin activity as leading indicators. High conviction BUY in early bull, first to cut in bear.

## CROSS-COIN CORRELATION RULES

1. **BTC dumps → everything dumps harder**. No exception. The beta hierarchy: BTC 1x → ETH 1.3x → Large alts 1.5-2x → Small alts 2-4x → Memes 3-5x
2. **ETH leads alt season**. When ETH/BTC starts rising from a bottom, alts follow within 2-8 weeks.
3. **SOL and ETH are inversely correlated in narrative**. When "SOL is the new ETH" narrative runs, ETH underperforms. When ETH ecosystem has catalysts, SOL underperforms.
4. **XRP and XLM move together** (payments narrative correlation ~0.7-0.8).
5. **Meme coins on the same chain move together**. BONK and WIF (both Solana) correlate at ~0.85. SHIB and PEPE (both Ethereum) correlate at ~0.7.
6. **DeFi tokens correlate with ETH** more than BTC. When ETH is strong, DeFi pumps. When ETH is weak, DeFi dies.
7. **AI tokens correlate with NVDA/tech stocks** more than with BTC. Watch NVDA earnings as a catalyst for FET, RENDER, TAO.
8. **Infrastructure tokens (LINK, GRT) lag the cycle**. They pump last, when DeFi activity is at peak and everyone needs oracles/indexing.
