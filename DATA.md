# PART 1: MACROECONOMIC NEWS & MARKET IMPACT

## 1. Federal Reserve & Monetary Policy

### FOMC Rate Decisions

The Federal Funds Rate cascades through the entire economy via the discount rate, prime rate, mortgage rates, corporate bond yields, and equity discount rates.

**Quantified Market Impact:**
- S&P 500 averages +0.5% move on FOMC days (absolute value), ~3x normal daily volatility
- March 2022 to July 2023: Fed raised from 0.00-0.25% to 5.25-5.50% (525 bps in 16 months). S&P 500 fell ~19.4% in 2022
- Historical pattern: In 12 months following FIRST rate cut, S&P 500 averages +14% when no recession, -15% when recession follows

### Sector Impact of Rising Rates

| Sector | Impact | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Financials (Banks) | POSITIVE | NIM expansion; JPMorgan NII rose from $52B (2021) to $89B (2023) |
| Insurance | POSITIVE | Higher reinvestment yields on bond portfolios |
| Technology (Growth) | NEGATIVE | Long-duration equity; Nasdaq fell 33% in 2022 vs S&P's 19% |
| Real Estate/REITs | NEGATIVE | Higher cap rates compress valuations; MSCI US REIT -25% in 2022 |
| Utilities | NEGATIVE | Bond proxies become less attractive vs treasuries |
| Consumer Discretionary | NEGATIVE | Higher borrowing costs reduce big-ticket spending |
| Energy | MIXED/POSITIVE | Rising rates often coincide with strong economy/inflation |
| Healthcare | DEFENSIVE | Inelastic demand; biotech (high growth, no earnings) hit harder |
| Consumer Staples | MILDLY NEGATIVE | Bond-proxy characteristics make them less attractive |
| Materials | MIXED | Depends on whether rates rise due to inflation (positive) or tightening (negative) |

### Sector Impact of Falling Rates
- Tech/Growth: Largest beneficiaries. Nasdaq rallied ~30% from Oct 2023 to Mar 2024
- Banks: Can be hurt (NIM compression). At near-zero rates, bank NII margins fell to multi-decade lows
- REITs: Rally strongly. VNQ gained ~17% in Q4 2023
- Homebuilders: XHB rallied ~65% from October 2023 lows to spring 2024

### Quantitative Easing (QE) vs Quantitative Tightening (QT)

**QE Impact on Markets:**
- QE1 (2008-2010): $1.75T. S&P 500 +68% from March 2009 low
- QE2 (2010-2011): $600B. S&P 500 +28%
- QE3 (2012-2014): ~$1.6T. S&P 500 +43%
- COVID QE (2020-2022): ~$4.8T. S&P 500 doubled from COVID low
- Every $1T of QE lowers 10Y yields by ~50-75bps (Fed staff research)

**QT Impact:**
- Tightens financial conditions, reduces bank reserves
- QT2 (Jun 2022+): $60B/month Treasuries + $35B/month MBS
- Estimated drag of ~25bps per $500B of QT (equivalent to small rate hike)

### Fed Speeches — Intraday Impact

**Speaker Ranking by Impact:**
1. Fed Chair (Powell) — Press conferences can move S&P 1-3% intraday
2. Vice Chair
3. NY Fed President (permanent FOMC voter)
4. Governors & Regional Presidents

**Key Language:**
- Hawkish: "Inflation remains too high," "further tightening may be needed" → equities sell, yields rise, dollar strengthens
- Dovish: "Restrictive enough," "risks becoming more balanced" → equities rally, yields fall, dollar weakens
- Powell Jackson Hole 2022 ("some pain") → S&P -3.4%
- Powell Jackson Hole 2024 ("time has come") → S&P +1.1%

## 2. Economic Indicators — Detailed Impact

### CPI / PPI (Inflation Data)

**Release:** CPI second week of month, 8:30 AM ET. Currently the SINGLE most market-moving data point.

**Market Sensitivity:** Average S&P 500 move on CPI days in 2022-2023: ~1.5% (vs ~0.7% normal days)

**Key Components:**
- Core CPI (ex-food/energy) matters more than headline to the Fed
- Shelter/OER (~36% of CPI basket) — lagging indicator
- Core Services ex-Housing ("supercore") — Powell flagged as THE key metric

| CPI Scenario | Winners | Losers |
|-------------|---------|--------|
| Hot (above expectations) | Energy, Commodities, Financials | Tech/Growth, REITs, Utilities, Consumer Disc |
| Cool (below expectations) | Tech/Growth, REITs, Homebuilders, Small-caps | Energy, Dollar-long trades |

### Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP)

**Release:** First Friday of every month, 8:30 AM ET

**Key Numbers:**
- Headline NFP (net jobs): 150K miss either direction moves markets 1-2%
- Average Hourly Earnings: Critical for wage-inflation concerns
- Revisions: Large downward revisions move markets as much as headline

**"Bad News Is Good News" Paradigm:**
- During inflation-fighting: Weak jobs = less Fed pressure = stocks rally
- During recession fears: Weak jobs = deterioration = stocks sell off
- The REGIME matters. Must identify current regime first

### GDP Reports

- Backward-looking; markets usually priced in the trajectory
- GDP composition matters: consumer-driven = "healthy," inventory-driven = "fragile"
- Atlanta Fed GDPNow is more market-moving than actual BEA release

### ISM Manufacturing / Services PMI

**Release:** Manufacturing 1st business day; Services 3rd business day of month
- 50 = expansion/contraction divide
- ISM Services MORE impactful (US economy ~77% services)
- Sub-50 readings sustained 3+ months: historically precedes recessions 70% of the time
- Key sub-indices: Prices Paid (inflation), New Orders (forward momentum), Employment

### Other Key Indicators

**Retail Sales:** Direct gauge of consumer spending (~70% of GDP). Control Group feeds into GDP PCE calculation.

**Housing Data:** Most interest-rate-sensitive sector. Every 100bp rise in mortgage rates reduces purchasing power ~12%.

**Consumer Confidence:** UMich 5-10Y inflation expectations component is critical — Fed explicitly monitors it.

**Jobless Claims:** Weekly (Thursday). 4-week MA below 250K = healthy. Sustained above 300K = recession signal.

## 3. Treasury Yields & Bond Market

### Yield Curve — Recession Prediction

**2Y/10Y Spread:**
- Inverted before EVERY recession since 1955, with one false positive (1966)
- Inverted July 2022, remained inverted for record 793 consecutive days
- The UN-inversion (steepening back to positive) is often the more immediate recession signal

**Key Rule:** Rising yields → Value stocks outperform Growth. Falling yields → Growth outperforms.

**Quantified:** Oct-Dec 2023, 10Y fell from 5.0% to 3.8%. Nasdaq +16%, Russell 2000 Growth +24%, vs S&P Value +10%.

### Bond Yields and Equity Multiples

**Equity Risk Premium (ERP)** = S&P 500 Earnings Yield − 10Y Treasury Yield
- Every 100bp move in 10Y corresponds to ~2-3 P/E point change (Goldman Sachs)
- At S&P EPS of ~$250, a 1-point P/E contraction = ~250 points (~5%)

- **Winners:** Financials, Energy, Materials, Industrials, Value
- **Losers:** Tech/Growth, REITs, Utilities, Long-duration Bonds

# PART 3: HOW PROFESSIONAL ANALYSTS READ THE MARKET

## 1. Market Breadth Indicators

### Advance/Decline Line
The A/D line tracks cumulative difference between advancing and declining issues. When S&P 500 makes new high but A/D line does not → rally is narrowing. This divergence preceded every major top since 1998-2000.

### New Highs vs New Lows
**Zweig Breadth Thrust:** When 10-day MA of NYSE advancing/(advancing+declining) moves from below 0.40 to above 0.615 within 10 days → one of the most reliable buy signals in history (near-perfect forward 6-12 month returns).

### Percentage of Stocks Above Moving Averages
- Below 20% on 50-day metric = washed-out conditions
- Reversal above 60% from <20% = durable low signal
- Above 90% = strong thrust (marks beginning, not end, of rallies)

## 2. Volume Analysis

### Accumulation/Distribution
When close is in upper half of range on high volume = institutional accumulation. Price making new highs while A/D volume declining = distribution (institutions selling into strength).

### On-Balance Volume (OBV)
OBV breakouts preceding price breakouts are among most reliable lead indicators. OBV trending higher while price bases = accumulation by informed money.

### Volume Profile / VWAP
- Point of Control (POC): price with most volume, acts as magnet
- High Volume Nodes: support/resistance
- Low Volume Nodes: price moves through quickly
- Stock above VWAP = being accumulated; below = distributed

## 3. Sentiment Indicators

### VIX (Volatility Index)
- Below 12: extreme complacency (dangerous)
- Above 40: capitulation signal
- Above 50: extreme panic
- VIX term structure inversion (backwardation) = acute fear
- VIX mean-reverts aggressively. Selling vol after spike >35-40 historically consistent

### Put/Call Ratio
- Equity-only put/call 5-day MA above 0.80: excessive bearishness → contrarian bullish
- Below 0.55: excessive bullishness → contrarian bearish

### AAII Sentiment Survey
- Bull-bear spread > +30: market underperforms next 6 months
- Bull-bear spread < -20: forward returns significantly above average
- Only works at EXTREMES

### Smart Money Index
Last 30 minutes of trading (smart money) vs first 30 minutes (emotional/retail). When SMI diverges from index → impending reversal.

## 4. Flow Analysis

### Dark Pool Activity
- Dark Pool Index (DIX) above 45%: historically bullish (institutions buying aggressively)
- DIX below 40%: bearish

### Options Flow — The Real Institutional Edge
- Unusual activity: volume exceeding open interest in single names
- Sweeps: aggressive multi-exchange fills = urgency and conviction
- Opening vs closing: opening activity more significant
- Near-dated options = catalyst play. LEAPS = longer-term thesis

### 13F Filings
- Filed quarterly by $100M+ managers (45-day lag)
- Crowding risk: when many hedge funds own same names, forced liquidations cascade
- Better for idea generation and crowding analysis than timing

### Insider Buying
- Insider buying is more informative than selling (they buy for only one reason: undervaluation)
- Cluster buying (3+ insiders) is strongest signal
- CEO/CFO purchases more informative than directors
- Dollar size matters. $2M purchase >> $20K purchase
- Insider buying at 52-week lows has highest predictive power

### Short Interest
- Above 10% of float: elevated. Above 20%: extreme squeeze potential
- Days to cover > 5: significant squeeze risk on any catalyst
- Rapidly increasing short interest into a basing stock = powerful contrarian setup

## 5. Earnings Season Playbook

### Earnings Reaction Matrix

| Scenario | Typical Reaction |
|----------|-----------------|
| Beat + Raise guidance | Strong multi-day rally |
| Beat + Maintain guidance | Modest positive, often fades |
| Beat + Lower guidance | Sell-off (guidance dominates) |
| Miss + Raise guidance | Mixed, often recovers |
| Miss + Lower guidance | Sharp multi-day decline |

### Post-Earnings Announcement Drift (PEAD)
Stocks with positive surprises drift higher for 60-90 days. Negative surprises drift lower. Effect strongest in first 5 days (~50% of total drift). Strongest when accompanied by revenue surprise + guidance raise + high volume + analyst upgrade.

### Forward Guidance Dominance
- 10% EPS beat + maintained guidance → 1-2% move
- Same beat + 5% guidance raise → 5-10% move
- Revenue beats weighted more heavily than EPS beats (revenue harder to manipulate)
- "Kitchen sink" quarter often marks the low

### Bellwether Contagion
- AAPL → all consumer electronics + semiconductor suppliers
- JPM (first bank to report) → sets tone for all financials
- FDX/UPS → proxies for consumer and economy
- WMT/TGT → sets tone for consumer

## 6. Seasonal Patterns

### Calendar Effects
- **January Barometer:** S&P direction in January predicts full year ~70-75% accuracy
- **Sell in May:** S&P returns ~7% Nov-Apr vs ~1.5% May-Oct historically
- **Worst month:** September (only month with negative average return)
- **Santa Rally:** Last 5 trading days of Dec + first 2 of Jan. ~75% win rate, avg +1.3%. Failure is a WARNING signal.

### Options Expiration
- Monthly OpEx (3rd Friday): "pin" effect toward max pain, less volatile
- Week AFTER OpEx: increased volatility as gamma cushion removed
- Quarterly "Triple Witching" (Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec): dramatic volume spikes

### End-of-Quarter Rebalancing
- Pension funds sell winners, buy bonds after strong equity quarters (final 3-5 days)
- Mutual fund "window dressing": buy winners, sell losers before quarter-end
- Russell reconstitution (late June): additions see buying pressure, deletions see selling

## 7. Risk Management — Rules from Top Traders

### Position Sizing
- **2% Rule:** Never risk more than 2% of portfolio on single trade. At 2%, need 35+ consecutive losers to draw down 50%.
- **Kelly Criterion:** f* = (bp - q) / b. NO professional uses full Kelly. Use Half Kelly or Quarter Kelly.
- **Risk Parity (Dalio):** Allocate based on equal risk contribution, not equal dollar amounts.

### Stop-Loss Strategies
- **ATR-Based (Professional Standard):** 2x ATR from entry. Adapts to volatility.
- **Chandelier Exit:** 3x ATR trailing stop below highest high since entry.
- **Percentage (O'Neil/CANSLIM):** 7-8% from buy point. Leading stocks that break out don't pull back >7-8%.
- **Time-Based:** Exit if no move in your favor within defined period.

### Top Trader Quotes
- Paul Tudor Jones: "Don't ever average losers."
- Druckenmiller: "How much you make when right and lose when wrong matters more than being right."
- Ed Seykota: "Risk no more than you can afford to lose, and risk enough so a win is meaningful."

### Correlation Management in Crises
- In crises, correlations converge to 1. Diversification fails when needed most.
- Use rolling 30-60 day correlation matrices. Rising cross-asset correlations → reduce gross exposure.
- Equity-bond correlation can turn positive in extreme inflation (as in 2022).

### Regime-Based Exposure
- S&P above rising 200-day MA: 80-100% invested
- Below 200-day MA: reduce to 50-60%
- Below declining 200-day MA: reduce to 20-40%
- Faber (2006): Long above 10-month SMA, cash below → captures most upside, avoids worst drawdowns

## 8. Capitulation Signals Checklist

When 4+ of these fire simultaneously, begin scaling in:
1. Volume spike: 2-3x the 50-day average
2. VIX above 40 (ideally 50+), term structure inverted
3. AAII bears above 50%, put/call above 1.2

5. Less than 10% of S&P 500 above 50-day MA
6. Intraday reversal on massive volume
7. Hedge fund closures / redemption suspensions

## 9. Behavioral Finance Exploits

### Fear/Greed Cycle (Howard Marks Framework)
Economy improves → earnings grow → stocks rise → optimism → leverage → valuation expansion → euphoria → bad investments funded → quality deteriorates → losses appear → sentiment shifts → selling → panic → capitulation → opportunities emerge → repeat.

**Action:** Increase exposure at stages 9-10 (capitulation). Decrease at stages 4-5 (euphoria).

### Anchoring Bias
- Analysts anchor price targets to current price (procyclical, not independent valuation)
- 52-week high creates underreaction — stocks near highs tend to outperform
- Stocks breaking through long-standing anchors with volume → accelerating moves

### Herding Risk
- Goldman's "Hedge Fund Crowding" basket underperforms during market stress
- Highest alpha comes from positions NOT widely held by institutional peers
- Check 13F crowding before new positions (WhaleWisdom)

- In bull markets: SOPR dipping to 1.0 and bouncing = healthy pullback
- In bear markets: SOPR rallying to 1.0 and rejecting = resistance
