# 1. BTC-Specific Patterns

## 1.1 Consolidation Patterns and What They Predict

### Pattern BTC-1: Tight Range Above the 200-Day SMA

- **Description:** When BTC consolidates in a tight range (less than 5% bandwidth) directly above the 200-day SMA for 2+ weeks, it signals accumulation before a directional move. Historically, the subsequent move is upward approximately 70% of the time.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~70% bullish resolution.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - October 2023: BTC consolidated at $27,000-$28,500 above the 200-day SMA for three weeks, then broke to $35,000 (+25%) within two weeks.
  - July 2020: BTC consolidated at $9,000-$9,500 above the 200-day SMA, then launched to $12,000 within a month.
- **When It Fails:** During macro risk-off events that override technical setups. The March 2020 COVID crash sliced through the 200-day SMA without warning, invalidating any consolidation setup.

### Pattern BTC-2: Bollinger Band Squeeze (Volatility Compression)

- **Description:** When the Bollinger Bands (20-period, 2 standard deviations) narrow to their tightest width in 6+ months, an explosive move follows within 1-2 weeks. The direction cannot be predicted by the squeeze alone, but the magnitude is reliable.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~90% for a 10%+ move within 14 days of the squeeze.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - September 2025: BTC traded $85,000-$90,000 with BB width narrowing to under $3,500 (the tightest since July). The subsequent move was a slide to $80,000 by month-end.
  - Late July 2025: A two-week sideways grind between $115,000-$120,000 produced a BB squeeze, followed by a volatility explosion.
  - February 2025: Range of $94,000-$98,000 tightened into a squeeze, followed by a slide to $80,000 by month-end.
- **When It Fails:** In periods of true market death (extreme low participation) where volume dries up so much that the expansion is trivial (sub-3%).

### Pattern BTC-3: Declining Volume Wedge (Rising Price, Falling Volume)

- **Description:** When BTC price makes higher highs but OBV (On-Balance Volume) makes lower highs, it signals weakening momentum despite price strength. This bearish divergence often precedes reversals of 15-30%.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~65-70% as a bearish signal on daily timeframes.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - November-December 2017: As BTC climbed toward $20,000, RSI posted a downward pattern. BTC subsequently lost 80%+ over 12 months.
  - April 2021: During the climb toward $65,000, RSI/OBV diverged bearishly. BTC crashed to $30,000 within two months.
- **When It Fails:** During parabolic blow-off tops where the trend is so strong that divergences can persist for weeks before resolving. Fading a parabolic move based on divergence alone has historically been costly.

### Pattern BTC-4: 200-Week Moving Average -- The Cycle Floor

- **Description:** Bitcoin has never sustained a close below the 200-week moving average in its history. This level has marked the absolute bottom of every bear market cycle.
- **Historical Win Rate:** 100% as cycle bottom support (across 4 cycles).
- **Specific Examples:**
  - 2015 bear market: BTC found support at ~$200, precisely at the 200-WMA.
  - 2018-2019 bear market: BTC bottomed at ~$3,200, right at the 200-WMA.
  - June 2022: BTC fell below the 200-WMA briefly (below $22,000) but reclaimed it by October 2023. This was the deepest and longest excursion below it.
- **When It Fails:** The June 2022 breach was the closest to a true failure. If a future bear market is driven by an existential threat (regulatory ban, protocol-level vulnerability), this support could break permanently.

### Pattern BTC-5: The 2.4x 200-Day SMA Cycle Top

- **Description:** Historical cycle tops have aligned with BTC reaching approximately 2.4x its 200-day SMA. When BTC trades at 2.4x or above, the market is in extreme overheated territory.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Identified tops in 2013, 2017, and 2021 cycles within 2 weeks of the peak.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - December 2017: BTC at ~2.4x the 200-day SMA when it peaked near $20,000.
  - November 2021: BTC at ~2.3x the 200-day SMA when it peaked near $69,000.
  - If this pattern holds, the current cycle top would project to approximately $184,600 based on the 200-day SMA trajectory.
- **When It Fails:** Institutional adoption via ETFs has flattened volatility by 55%, potentially dampening the multiplier in future cycles. The 2024-2025 cycle showed more muted peak multipliers.

### Pattern BTC-6: Golden Cross / Death Cross (50/200-Day MA Crossover)

- **Description:** A golden cross (50-day MA crosses above 200-day MA) signals bullish momentum; a death cross signals bearish momentum. These are lagging indicators but have directional significance.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~55-60% as standalone signals; ~70-80% when combined with RSI and MACD confirmation.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - April 2019 Golden Cross: BTC at ~$5,000, rallied to $12,000 within months.
  - May 2020 Golden Cross: Preceded the rally from ~$9,000 to the $69,000 ATH in November 2021.
  - Death Cross in 2022: Returns in the first three weeks after are evenly split between gains and losses. Two to three months later, average recoveries range from 15% to 26%.
- **When It Fails:** In choppy, sideways markets, multiple false crosses can occur (whipsaws). The signals are significantly lagging -- by the time a golden cross forms, 30-50% of the move may already be over.

## 1.2 Weekend vs. Weekday Behavior

### Pattern BTC-7: The Monday Pump

- **Description:** Bitcoin returns on Mondays are statistically higher than other days. Monday has positive effects on both return and transaction volume.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Statistically significant positive Monday returns across multiple academic studies (2014-2024 data).
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Academic research using ANOVA and Kruskal-Wallis tests confirms Monday outperformance.
  - Weekend momentum strategies consistently outperform weekday counterparts, with mean daily returns on weekends often doubling those on weekdays.
- **When It Fails:** During the COVID-19 period (March-June 2020), there was a shift to negative Monday returns. Major macro events override the day-of-week effect.

### Pattern BTC-8: Weekend Volatility Exploitation

- **Description:** Weekends feature lower trading volume and institutional participation, making prices more susceptible to large moves. Smaller orders can move the market more significantly during weekends.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Altcoins exhibit a stronger weekend momentum differential than major coins.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Weekend price gaps frequently create CME futures gaps that then fill during the week (see Pattern BTC-12).
  - Saturday has negative effects on transaction volume, creating thinner order books.
- **When It Fails:** As crypto matures and 24/7 institutional trading desks proliferate, the weekend effect has been gradually diminishing since 2023.

### Pattern BTC-9: Sunday Dip Pattern

- **Description:** Sunday late afternoon (UTC) often sees localized selling pressure as Asian markets prepare for Monday and weekend retail traders close positions. This creates a micro-dip that often reverses by Monday morning.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Mixed findings -- positive returns were generally observed on Sundays in bull markets, but negative in bear/uncertain markets.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - The pattern is most pronounced during risk-off periods when weekend holders are nervous about Monday gaps in traditional markets.
- **When It Fails:** During strong bullish momentum, Sunday dips get immediately bought. In strong bear markets, Sunday dips accelerate into Monday dumps.

## 1.3 Month-End / Quarter-End Patterns

### Pattern BTC-10: Last Trading Days of the Month -- Strongest Period

- **Description:** Contrary to equity market month-end rebalancing effects, the last trading days of the month are the strongest-performing period for Bitcoin ETPs. Returns in the first days of the new month are positive but more modest, and the middle portion of the month tends to generate negative returns.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Research on Bitcoin ETPs confirms this pattern, though sample sizes remain limited.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Institutional rebalancing flows in crypto do not operate the same way as in traditional equity and bond markets.
- **When It Fails:** In strong bear markets, month-end becomes a period of accelerated selling as portfolio managers crystallize losses.

### Pattern BTC-11: Quarter-End Rebalancing Selling Pressure

- **Description:** Quarter-end triggers profit-taking and portfolio rebalancing in Bitcoin ETFs, creating short-term selling pressure. This is rotation, not structural outflows.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Consistent pattern since ETF launch in January 2024.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Q1 2025 end: Bitcoin ETFs posted outflows driven by quarter-end rebalancing, ending a four-week inflow streak.
  - Q4 2024 end: Similar rebalancing outflows observed.
- **When It Fails:** When quarterly performance has been negative, there is less profit-taking pressure and potentially inflows from rebalancing into the now-underweight allocation.

## 1.4 CME Futures Gap Fills

### Pattern BTC-12: CME Gap Fill -- The 85% Rule

- **Description:** CME Bitcoin futures trade Mon-Fri only. Weekend BTC price moves create "gaps" on the CME chart. Historically, approximately 85% of these gaps eventually fill, with the price returning to the gap zone.
- **Historical Win Rate:** 85% overall fill rate. Gaps under $700 have a 92% fill rate within 30 trading days. ~67% of gaps fill within 48 hours.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Out of 80 identified Bitcoin CME gaps in one study, all but one were ultimately filled.
  - August 2025: A weekend surge left a gap that was subsequently traded toward.
  - A $695 gap in 2024 remained open for over a month amid Fed rate uncertainty (exception case).
- **When It Fails:** Larger gaps (>$1,000) during strong trending markets may take months or even years to fill. Some gaps formed during major paradigm shifts (ETF approval gap, for example) remain unfilled for extended periods. Macroeconomic shocks and regulatory developments can delay or prevent fills.

### Pattern BTC-13: CME Gap Direction Bias

- **Description:** Upside gaps (weekend pump) tend to fill faster than downside gaps (weekend dump), because the market has a natural tendency to retrace weekend euphoria more quickly than weekend fear.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~70% of upside gaps fill within one week.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - The gap-fill arbitrage strategy between CME and spot exchanges like Binance is a well-documented institutional trade.
- **When It Fails:** During sustained momentum (e.g., post-ETF-approval rally in January 2024), upside gaps may not fill for weeks.

## 1.5 Options Expiry Effects

### Pattern BTC-14: Max Pain Gravitational Pull

- **Description:** As options expiry approaches, BTC price tends to gravitate toward the "max pain" strike -- the price at which the greatest number of options contracts expire worthless, minimizing payouts. Market makers' delta-hedging activity creates this gravitational force.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Descriptive rather than perfectly predictive. The effect is strongest in the 24-48 hours before expiry and most pronounced for large quarterly expirations.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - December 2025: $27 billion in Bitcoin and Ether options set for year-end reset, with prices pinning near max pain levels.
  - BTC and ETH were "pinned at max pain" as $2.2 billion in options expired during a macro storm.
- **When It Fails:** When strong directional flows overwhelm the hedging activity. Max pain has little effect when a single large player takes a directional bet that outweighs the distributed hedging pressure.

### Pattern BTC-15: Options Expiry Volatility Cycle

- **Description:** Volatility compresses into expiry as traders scale back directional bets, then expands after settlement. The pattern is: pre-expiry compression -> expiry pin -> post-expiry directional move.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Consistent pattern. Volatility increases by 5-8% for weekly expiry, 10-15% for monthly, and 18-25% for quarterly expiry.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Quarterly expiry dates (last Friday of March, June, September, December) consistently produce the largest post-expiry moves.
- **When It Fails:** When expiry coincides with a major macro catalyst (FOMC, CPI), the macro event dominates and the expiry effect is subsumed.

### Pattern BTC-16: Pin Risk at Round Strikes

- **Description:** BTC price tends to "pin" at round-number strike prices ($50,000, $60,000, $70,000, etc.) near expiry, particularly when large open interest is concentrated at those strikes.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Observable in ~60% of large monthly/quarterly expirations.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - BTC has repeatedly pinned near $100,000, $90,000, and $80,000 strikes during 2025 monthly expirations.
- **When It Fails:** When open interest is distributed across many strikes rather than concentrated at one level.

## 1.6 Pre-Halving and Post-Halving Micro-Patterns

### Pattern BTC-17: Pre-Halving Rally Frontrunning

- **Description:** Markets increasingly front-run the halving event. In 2016, BTC rose 13% in the 30 days before halving. In 2020, it rose 25%. In 2024, the entire rally happened before the halving (BTC hit ATH of $73,750 on March 14, 2024 -- a full month before the April halving), with only 1% gain in the final 30 days.
- **Historical Win Rate:** The pre-halving pump has occurred 3/3 times, but the timing is accelerating (rally happens earlier each cycle).
- **Specific Examples:**
  - 2016 halving (July 9): BTC rose from ~$450 to ~$650 in the 6 months before.
  - 2020 halving (May 11): BTC rose from ~$7,000 to ~$9,000 in the 30 days before.
  - 2024 halving (April 20): BTC rose from ~$42,000 to $73,750 ATH on March 14, then actually dipped before halving.
- **When It Fails:** The 2024 pattern suggests the halving narrative is now fully priced in before the event. Future halvings may see "buy the rumor, sell the news" intensify.

### Pattern BTC-18: Post-Halving Dip (The "Sell the News" Window)

- **Description:** A 5-15% dip occurs in the 2-4 weeks immediately following the halving as speculative capital exits after the event catalyst passes.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Observed in 2/3 halvings (2020 and 2024).
- **Specific Examples:**
  - 2024 halving: BTC was ~$63,000 on halving day. Spiked to $66,000 in 3 days, then dropped to $58,568 by May 1 (-10%).
  - 2020 halving: Minor dip from $9,000 to $8,500 in the week after.
- **When It Fails:** If macro conditions are overwhelmingly bullish (e.g., massive ETF inflows), the dip may be shallow and immediately bought.

### Pattern BTC-19: The 12-18 Month Post-Halving Supercycle

- **Description:** The major price appreciation occurs not at the halving but 12-18 months later. This is the well-known macro pattern, but the micro-signal is tracking the transition from the post-halving dip into the initial markup phase.
- **Historical Win Rate:** 3/3 cycles showed massive gains in the 12-18 months after halving.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - 2020 halving (May 2020): 12 months later, BTC at ~$57,000 (+527%).
  - 2016 halving (July 2016): 18 months later, BTC at ~$20,000 (+2,900%).
  - 2024 halving: Post-halving price action tracked almost exactly opposite to 2020, suggesting ETF-driven demand dynamics may have fundamentally altered the pattern.
- **When It Fails:** The 2024 cycle broke the traditional pattern because ETF demand pulled forward much of the price appreciation to before the halving. The halving narrative's diminishing returns may make this pattern less reliable in future cycles.

### Pattern BTC-20: Miner Capitulation Post-Halving

- **Description:** After halving, inefficient miners are forced to sell BTC holdings or shut down rigs as revenue drops 50%. This creates 4-8 weeks of elevated selling pressure from miner wallets, observable on-chain. The Hash Ribbon indicator captures this: when the 30-day MA of network power crosses below the 60-day MA, it signals miner capitulation. Historically, the end of miner capitulation marks a local bottom.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Periods of negative 90-day hashrate growth have delivered positive 180-day bitcoin returns 77% of the time.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Post-2024 halving: BTC hashrate dropped 15% from October high, with miner capitulation dragging into ~60 days. Mining difficulty saw the seventh negative adjustment in eight periods.
  - Post-2020 halving: Network power dropped ~25%, then recovered, with the recovery coinciding with the beginning of the bull run.
- **When It Fails:** When miners have sufficient cash reserves or hedging strategies to avoid forced selling. Miners pivoting to AI/HPC operations (like Riot Platforms in 2025) can sell Bitcoin for strategic reasons unrelated to mining economics, creating false capitulation signals.

## 1.7 On-Chain and Derivatives Signals

### Pattern BTC-21: MVRV Z-Score Cycle Extremes

- **Description:** The MVRV Z-Score compares Market Value to Realized Value. Z-score above 6.9 = overbought (sell zone). Z-score below 0.1 = oversold (buy zone). Historical accuracy reaches 94% for identifying market cycle tops and bottoms.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Has identified cycle highs within 2 weeks of the peak across all major cycles.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - December 2017: MVRV ratio soared to 4.72, Z-score exceeded 7. Market topped within days.
  - Spring 2021: MVRV ratio reached 3.96, Z-score exceeded 7. Market entered correction.
  - November 2022 bottom: Z-score near 0, marking the absolute cycle low.
- **When It Fails:** During extended sideways markets, the Z-score can hover in neutral territory for months without providing actionable signals. The metric is a macro-cycle tool, not a short-term trading indicator.

### Pattern BTC-22: NUPL (Net Unrealized Profit/Loss) Sentiment Bands

- **Description:** NUPL measures total paper profits/losses held by Bitcoin investors. Sentiment bands: >0.75 = Euphoria (sell), 0.5-0.75 = Greed, 0.25-0.5 = Optimism, 0-0.25 = Hope/Fear, <0 = Capitulation (buy).
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~85% accuracy when NUPL enters extreme zones (>0.75 or <0).
- **Specific Examples:**
  - RUP (Relative Unrealized Profit) spikes above 0.75 have historically indicated tops.
  - When RUL (Relative Unrealized Loss) exceeds RUP, it has been historically indicative of market bottoms.
- **When It Fails:** Can give premature signals in strong trends. NUPL entered the "Euphoria" zone multiple times during the 2021 double-top, with the first signal coming months before the actual top.

### Pattern BTC-23: NVT Ratio (Network Value to Transactions)

- **Description:** The NVT ratio is the "P/E ratio of Bitcoin." High NVT (70-100) = BTC is overvalued relative to transaction throughput. Low NVT (30-50) = BTC is undervalued. Think of it as: is the market cap justified by actual network usage?
- **Historical Win Rate:** High NVT values have historically coincided with market tops. Low values coincide with bottoms and bull market beginnings.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - NVT spiked before the 2017 crash and the 2021 correction.
  - NVT was low during accumulation phases in 2019 and late 2022.
- **When It Fails:** Changes in Bitcoin's use case (e.g., Lightning Network adoption, Layer 2 activity) can distort on-chain transaction volume, making NVT less reliable as off-chain transactions grow.

### Pattern BTC-24: Funding Rate Mean Reversion

- **Description:** Extremely positive funding rates (>0.1% per 8 hours) on perpetual futures signal crowded longs -- contrarian bearish. Extremely negative funding rates (<-0.05% per 8 hours) signal crowded shorts -- contrarian bullish. Arbitrage capital forces rates back to the 0.01% baseline.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Extreme funding rates are a meaningful contrarian signal, but timing is imprecise. Spikes are typically short-lived.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - April 2021: Funding rates spiked above 0.1% before the crash from $65,000 to $30,000.
  - June 2022: Deeply negative funding preceded the local bottom near $17,500.
- **When It Fails:** Funding rates can persist in positive or negative territory for extended periods without reversals. During the November 2024 rally, positive funding persisted for weeks as genuine demand absorbed the cost.

### Pattern BTC-25: Open Interest Divergence

- **Description:** When BTC price rises but open interest falls, it signals that the rally is driven by short covering, not new longs. This type of rally tends to be shorter-lived. Conversely, rising price + rising OI = genuine new demand.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~65% as a standalone signal for rally sustainability.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Rising OI + negative funding = short squeeze setup (shorts are paying to hold, meaning crowded short positions).
  - Falling OI + rising price = short covering rally, likely to stall.
- **When It Fails:** In thin-liquidity environments, OI can drop simply because exchanges are reducing leverage limits or positions are being transferred, not because of genuine de-risking.

### Pattern BTC-26: Exchange Reserve Depletion (Supply Shock)

- **Description:** Bitcoin exchange reserves have fallen to 2.4 million BTC (14.5% of supply), the lowest since 2018. Institutions are outbidding new supply at a 2-to-1 ratio. Declining exchange reserves, when combined with increasing demand, create "supply shock" conditions.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Historically, declining exchange reserves have preceded supply-driven price expansions.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs accumulated 936,830+ BTC since January 2024 launch.
  - MicroStrategy holds 673,783 BTC with no near-term sell mandate.
  - Average daily outflow of 1,178 BTC over recent months.
  - Long-term holders grabbed 75,000 BTC ($3.2 billion) in just ten days in late 2025.
- **When It Fails:** CryptoQuant has noted that exchange reserve decline alone does not guarantee supply shock -- reserves can decline due to cold storage migration, OTC desk movements, or simply exchange consolidation. Context matters.

### Pattern BTC-27: Fear & Greed Index Extremes

- **Description:** The Fear & Greed Index (0-100) is a composite of volatility, volume, social media, dominance, and trends. Readings below 20 ("Extreme Fear") have been followed by positive returns over subsequent 30/60/90 days approximately 85% of the time, with an average 30-day return of 18.7%.
- **Historical Win Rate:** 85% positive 30-day forward returns from "Extreme Fear" readings.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - November 2022: Index hit 10. BTC subsequently rallied over 100% within six months.
  - March 2020: Index hit single digits. BTC rallied from $3,800 to $60,000+ over 12 months.
  - February 2026: Index hit 8 as BTC crashed below $76,000.
- **When It Fails:** Extreme fear can persist for extended periods. The index can stay below 20 for weeks during sustained bear markets (e.g., May-July 2022). It's a sentiment indicator, not a timing tool.

### Pattern BTC-28: Liquidation Cascade Capitulation Bottom

- **Description:** When liquidation cascades exceed $2+ billion in 24 hours with 85%+ being long liquidations, this typically marks a local bottom. The pattern: initial shock -> margin calls -> forced closures -> liquidity providers pull back -> self-reinforcing cascade -> exhaustion -> recovery wick.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Large long liquidation events have preceded 1-4 week bounces approximately 75% of the time.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - October 2025: $3.21 billion liquidated in a single minute; $9.89 billion total over 14 hours.
  - February 2026: 335,000 traders liquidated in one day, $2.2 billion gone in 24 hours. BTC fell below $76,000 then rebounded.
  - November 2025: $1.9 billion liquidated in four hours (85% long liquidations).
- **When It Fails:** If the liquidation cascade is driven by a fundamental shift (exchange collapse like FTX), the "capitulation bottom" may be a false floor with further downside ahead.

### Pattern BTC-29: Whale Exchange Inflow Warning

- **Description:** When large wallets (100+ BTC) consistently send BTC to exchanges over multiple days, it signals distribution/preparation to sell. Single transfers are noise; 3+ similar moves from different whales over multiple days confirms the pattern.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Sustained institutional exchange inflows have preceded 10-20% corrections approximately 70% of the time.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Pre-crash exchange inflows are consistently detected by on-chain analytics firms (Glassnode, CryptoQuant, Nansen).
  - Conversely, sustained exchange outflows to cold storage signal accumulation -- the 2023-2024 accumulation phase was clearly visible through this metric.
- **When It Fails:** OTC desks use exchanges as intermediaries without the intent to market-sell. Large inflows can be for collateral purposes (lending, derivatives margin) rather than selling.

### Pattern BTC-30: ETF Flow Momentum

- **Description:** Bitcoin spot ETF daily net inflows/outflows have become the single most important short-term demand signal since January 2024. Multi-day streaks of net inflows (5+ consecutive days) correlate with sustained price appreciation. Multi-day outflows signal near-term weakness.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Strong correlation since ETF launch. BlackRock IBIT alone has taken in $62+ billion.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - March 2024: Highest inflow period coincided with BTC's ATH of $73,750.
  - October 2024: $3.2 billion in weekly ETF inflows during "Uptober" rally.
  - Q1 2025 end: ETFs posted outflows driven by quarter-end rebalancing.
- **When It Fails:** ETF flows can be hedged (creation for basis trade, not directional). Price can fall on big inflows if they reflect hedged creations, and rise on outflows if driven by profit-taking into tight supply.

### Pattern BTC-31: Wyckoff Accumulation Spring

- **Description:** The Wyckoff accumulation pattern features distinct phases. The "Spring" or shakeout phase (Phase C) -- where price breaks below the established trading range to trap bears -- is the final bear trap before markup begins. Identifying the Spring in real-time is the holy grail of bottom-fishing.
- **Historical Win Rate:** The Wyckoff framework correctly identified the 2021 distribution top and the 2022 accumulation bottom.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - July 2021: Spring at $30,100, followed by rally to $36,600.
  - June 2022: Selling climax (SC) marked the stopping of the downtrend at $17,600. The Spring occurred at $15,476 on November 21, 2022, which held as the absolute bottom.
- **When It Fails:** Wyckoff patterns are subjective -- different analysts will draw different phase boundaries. The pattern can be "invalidated" and redefined retroactively, reducing its predictive utility in real-time.

### Pattern BTC-32: The 10 AM UTC Dump

- **Description:** Bitcoin frequently experiences a sell-off around 10:00 AM EST (15:00 UTC) as U.S. market makers and institutional desks begin rebalancing positions after the equity market opens. This has been attributed to market makers like Jane Street hedging ETF positions.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Observable pattern in 2024-2025 data.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - The pattern is most pronounced on days with significant ETF activity and equity market volatility.
- **When It Fails:** On days with strong bullish catalysts (positive CPI, Fed dovish surprise), the 10 AM dip gets immediately absorbed.

# 2. Altcoin Patterns

## 2.1 ETH/BTC Ratio and Alt Season Signals

### Pattern ALT-1: ETH/BTC Ratio Above 0.07 -- Alt Season Trigger

- **Description:** When ETH/BTC rises above 0.07, it signals Ethereum outperformance vs. Bitcoin, which historically precedes broader altcoin rallies. ETH/BTC has historically fluctuated between 0.02 and 0.12.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Crossing above 0.07 has preceded alt seasons in 2017 and 2021.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - 2018 peak: ETH/BTC reached ~0.084.
  - 2021 peak: ETH/BTC reached ~0.087.
  - DeFi Summer 2020: ETH/BTC broke above 0.07, triggering massive alt rotation.
- **When It Fails:** In 2024-2025, ETH has underperformed BTC despite broader market strength due to ETF capital flowing into BTC-only products. The ETH/BTC ratio fell below 0.03, potentially breaking this historical indicator.

### Pattern ALT-2: ETH/BTC 250-Day Moving Average Crossover

- **Description:** When ETH/BTC breaks above its 250-day moving average, it signals a regime shift toward Ethereum and altcoin outperformance. This is a slower, more reliable signal than the absolute 0.07 threshold.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Crossovers marked the beginning of alt seasons in early 2017 and DeFi Summer 2020.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - The September 2022 Merge announcement lifted ETH/BTC from 0.05 to a near-record high.
- **When It Fails:** Crypto-specific structural changes (e.g., Bitcoin ETF creating a "Bitcoin-only" asset class) can decouple ETH/BTC from broader altcoin health.

### Pattern ALT-3: BTC Dominance Threshold Framework

- **Description:** BTC dominance levels signal altcoin rotation phases: >65% = altcoin suppression, 60-65% = transition zone, 55-60% = early altcoin momentum, 45-55% = active alt season, <45% = peak alt dominance.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Dominance breaking below 58-60% has triggered sustained altcoin rallies historically.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - 2017: Dominance collapsed from 86.3% to 38% over six months. Ethereum went from $8 to $1,400 (+17,400%).
  - 2021: Dominance dropped from 70% to 38%. Solana gained 20,000%.
  - January 2018: Dominance hit 38%, one of the lowest ever recorded, marking peak altcoin mania.
- **When It Fails:** The 2024-2025 cycle saw BTC dominance remain elevated (55-65%) even during price rallies, suggesting ETF-driven institutional capital may permanently keep BTC dominance higher than in previous cycles.

### Pattern ALT-4: Altcoin Season Index (ASI) Confirmation

- **Description:** If 75%+ of the top 50 altcoins (excluding stablecoins) outperform BTC over a rolling 90-day period, it confirms Altcoin Season. If only 25% or fewer outperform, it's Bitcoin Season.
- **Historical Win Rate:** The ASI has correctly identified all major alt seasons and Bitcoin seasons since its inception.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Track via blockchaincenter.net or similar dashboards.
  - The index hit "Altcoin Season" status in January 2018, May 2021, and November 2021.
- **When It Fails:** The index is backward-looking (90-day window). By the time it confirms "Altcoin Season," much of the initial rotation may have already occurred.

## 2.2 BTC-to-Alt Rotation Timing

### Pattern ALT-5: The BTC Consolidation -> Alt Rotation Lag

- **Description:** After a strong BTC rally, it consolidates sideways. During this consolidation, capital that realized gains in BTC rotates into altcoins. The lag is not a fixed time period but phase-based: (1) BTC pumps, alts lag. (2) BTC consolidates, large-cap alts begin outperforming. (3) BTC stays flat, mid/small-cap alts rally.
- **Historical Win Rate:** This rotation pattern has occurred in every bull cycle.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Q4 2020: BTC rallied from $10,000 to $40,000 (Oct-Jan). ETH didn't begin its outperformance until January-February 2021.
  - Q1 2024: BTC rallied to ATH in March, but broad alt rotation didn't begin until Q4 2024.
- **When It Fails:** In 2024-2025, the traditional BTC -> large-cap alt -> small-cap alt rotation was disrupted. ETF capital stayed in BTC and did not rotate into altcoins. The "investable altcoin market" has contracted into a top-heavy pyramid.

### Pattern ALT-6: BTC Crash -- Alt Beta Amplification

- **Description:** When BTC dumps, altcoins dump harder. Most altcoins have a beta of 1.3-2.5x relative to BTC during crashes. Small-cap alts can have betas of 3-5x.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Consistent across all market drawdowns.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - February 2026 crash: BTC fell below $76,000. ETH dropped 24% in a week (higher beta). Total crypto market cap shrank from $4.38T to $2.42T.
  - October 2025 crash: BTC dumped while altcoins took even sharper losses.
- **When It Fails:** Specific altcoins with strong narrative catalysts can temporarily decouple. During the March 2023 banking crisis, BTC rallied 20.5% while many alts lagged -- the "safe haven" within crypto.

### Pattern ALT-7: Defensive Altcoins During Crashes

- **Description:** Not all alts dump equally. Exchange tokens (BNB), stablecoin-adjacent chains (TRX), and tokens with regulatory tailwinds (XRP) tend to show lower drawdowns.
- **Historical Win Rate:** BNB, TRX, and XRP have consistently shown lower beta during crashes since 2023.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - BNB: Fell from ATH of $1,369 to $834-886 (a 11-32% decline vs. BTC's larger percentage drop) due to exchange utility and token burns.
  - TRX: Down just 1% YTD during the 2026 crash, outpacing BTC's sharper drop.
  - XRP: Dropped 15-20% but outperformed BTC in relative terms during broad crashes, boosted by ETF prospects and regulatory wins.
- **When It Fails:** Exchange tokens are exposed to exchange-specific risk (see FTT/FTX collapse). Regulatory shifts can instantly remove the "defensive" quality.

### Pattern ALT-8: Post-Crash Alt Recovery -- The Long Tail Problem

- **Description:** After crashes, only the top 10-20 alts by market cap reliably recover. Altcoins outside the top 10 increasingly fail to recover when Bitcoin rebounds. The "investable altcoin market" has contracted into a top-heavy pyramid where new liquidity concentrates in majors.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Structural trend observed since 2022, accelerating in 2024-2025.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - The long tail of altcoins gets "brief, thin narrative pops that fade within weeks."
  - Many altcoins from the 2021 cycle (e.g., mid-cap DeFi tokens) never recovered to their highs despite BTC reaching new ATHs.
- **When It Fails:** Specific narrative catalysts (AI narrative in 2023-2024, meme coin mania in 2024) can temporarily revive long-tail tokens.

## 2.3 Exchange Listing Patterns

### Pattern ALT-9: Binance Listing Pump and Dump

- **Description:** Tokens listed on Binance pump 87% on average at launch but dump 98% of the time afterward, losing an average of 70% of value post-listing. 37% of tokens hit their all-time high at listing and never surpass it.
- **Historical Win Rate:** 98% dump rate post-listing. Only 5 out of 31 recently studied listings saw price increases (ORDI, JUP, WIF, JTO, MEME -- primarily meme coins or projects without significant VC backing).
- **Specific Examples:**
  - New Binance listings launch at high fully diluted values, averaging over $4.2 billion (sometimes up to $11 billion).
  - A portfolio investing equally in each new Binance listing would be down over 18% in 6 months.
  - CEX listings trigger a 54% price surge on average at launch. 89% dump post-listing with average 52% price decline.
- **When It Fails:** Community-driven tokens without VC unlock overhang (like meme coins) can buck the pattern. Tokens with genuine product-market fit (rare) can sustain post-listing levels.

### Pattern ALT-10: Listing Announcement Front-Run

- **Description:** The real money is made in the announcement-to-listing window, not post-listing. When Binance announces a listing, the token typically pumps 30-100% on other exchanges before the Binance listing goes live. Post-listing is distribution.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~80% of tokens pump on listing announcement before dumping post-listing.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Historically, insiders and bots front-run listing announcements by monitoring Binance API endpoints and social channels.
- **When It Fails:** When the listing is already widely expected or priced in. Tokens that have already pumped significantly on listing rumors may dump on the actual announcement ("buy the rumor, sell the news").

### Pattern ALT-11: CEX Listing Tier Effect

- **Description:** The magnitude of the listing pump correlates with exchange tier. Binance listing > Coinbase listing > OKX listing > smaller exchanges. However, the dump post-listing is also proportional to the pump.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Binance listings produce the largest pumps and the most reliable subsequent dumps.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Pump-and-dump schemes on Binance have approximately 29% of the average return rate compared to those on Yobit, suggesting that more liquid exchanges see smaller manipulated pumps.
- **When It Fails:** Coinbase listings can produce sustained pumps when they unlock access for U.S. retail investors who couldn't previously access the token.

## 2.4 Token Unlock Patterns

### Pattern ALT-12: Pre-Unlock Price Decline (7-14 Days Before)

- **Description:** Markets front-run large token unlocks. Prices typically begin declining 7-14 days before a major unlock as traders anticipate the new supply. The larger the unlock relative to circulating supply, the greater the pre-unlock selling pressure.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Consistent pattern for unlocks representing >5% of circulating supply.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Large VC-backed tokens with cliff unlocks see the most pronounced pre-unlock declines.
  - Track upcoming unlocks via TokenUnlocks.app, CoinMarketCap, or CryptoRank.
- **When It Fails:** If the unlock goes to "long-term partners or ecosystem use" rather than liquid investors, the selling pressure may not materialize. In bull markets, demand can absorb the new supply.

### Pattern ALT-13: Cliff Unlock vs. Linear Unlock Impact

- **Description:** Cliff unlocks (large one-time releases) generate concentrated volatility around specific dates. Linear unlocks (continuous daily/weekly releases) create predictable but constant selling pressure that dampens price appreciation.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Cliff unlocks produce more tradeable volatility events; linear unlocks produce persistent drag.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Tokens with cliff unlocks: Arbitrum (ARB), Optimism (OP), Aptos (APT) have all seen pre-cliff-unlock price declines of 10-25%.
- **When It Fails:** When the unlock recipients are long-term believers who hold rather than sell. Not all unlocked tokens are immediately sold.

### Pattern ALT-14: Post-Unlock Withdrawal Pattern

- **Description:** DeFi protocols with significant inorganic TVL from airdrop farmers experience outsized withdrawal transactions vs. deposits immediately post-snapshot or post-unlock.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Consistent pattern across DeFi protocol launches.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Post-airdrop user engagement drops significantly, especially for StarkNet and zkSync.
  - Airdrop day sees significant DAU spikes that reverse within 1-2 weeks.
- **When It Fails:** Protocols that provide genuine utility and yield can retain users post-airdrop (e.g., Uniswap retained meaningful TVL post-UNI airdrop).

## 2.5 Airdrop Farming Effects

### Pattern ALT-15: Airdrop-Driven TVL Inflation

- **Description:** Pre-airdrop TVL growth is often inorganic -- driven by farmers seeking eligibility rather than genuine protocol usage. A gradual TVL increase after an airdrop highlights genuine confidence, while a sharp post-airdrop TVL decline signals the activity was purely mercenary.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Post-airdrop TVL declines occur in >80% of DeFi protocol launches.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - LayerZero's 2024 sybil purge eliminated over 800,000 addresses, setting the precedent for aggressive anti-farming measures.
  - Gas costs for multi-chain farming strategies range $50-$200 in total across L2s.
- **When It Fails:** Some protocols (e.g., Aave, Uniswap) have maintained and grown TVL despite initial farming-driven inflation. Protocols with genuine product-market fit can convert farmers into users.

### Pattern ALT-16: Airdrop Token Dump Pattern

- **Description:** The vast majority of airdrop recipients sell within 48-72 hours of claiming. This creates a predictable dump followed by stabilization. The alpha is in buying the post-dump stabilization level.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~80% of airdropped tokens decline 30-60% in the first week after distribution.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Airdrop farming economics: High gas costs ($5-15 per Ethereum mainnet tx, plus $50-200 total across L2s) create a breakeven threshold that forces small farmers to sell immediately.
- **When It Fails:** High-demand airdrops with limited supply (e.g., early Uniswap airdrop in 2020) can appreciate post-distribution in strong bull markets.

- **Specific Examples:**

# 3. Volume and Liquidity Patterns

### Pattern VOL-1: Volume Climax Capitulation Signature

- **Description:** A steep price drop paired with a volume surge exceeding 3x the 20-day average volume signals seller exhaustion (capitulation). Once a true volume climax occurs, average rebounds retrace 50-70% of the prior parabolic drop.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Low failure rate when volume exceeds 3x average. ~75% of true capitulation events (volume spike + price spike bottom) produce 50%+ retracement.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - March 2020 COVID crash: BTC volume spiked to record levels at $3,800. Rebound to $10,000+ within two months (163% retrace of the drop).
  - June 2022: Volume climax near $17,500 marked the cycle low.
- **When It Fails:** If the capitulation is the first leg of a multi-wave decline (e.g., LUNA collapse cascading into broader market), the "climax" may just be the beginning.

### Pattern VOL-2: Declining Volume in Uptrend -- Bearish Warning

- **Description:** When price makes higher highs but volume makes lower highs, the uptrend is losing momentum. This is one of the most reliable bearish divergence signals. On-Balance Volume (OBV) diverging from price is the formal indicator.
- **Historical Win Rate:** 65-70% accuracy for predicting a meaningful correction (10%+) on daily timeframes.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - November-December 2017: BTC climbed to $20,000 on declining volume. 80%+ crash followed.
  - April 2021: Rising price to $65,000 with OBV divergence. 50%+ crash followed.
- **When It Fails:** In strong parabolic moves driven by new market participants (ETF-driven 2024 rally), declining volume can persist for weeks before the divergence resolves. Taking a position against the higher time frame trend based on divergence alone is risky.

### Pattern VOL-3: Volume Confirmation of Breakout

- **Description:** A valid breakout from a consolidation range should be accompanied by volume at least 1.5x the 20-day average. Breakouts on low volume have a high failure rate. The most reliable signal: genuine breakouts show increasing volume + declining volume on the retest pullback.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Breakouts with volume confirmation succeed ~70% of the time vs. ~40% without.
- **When It Fails:** In the ETF era (2024+), significant BTC price moves can occur with lower on-chain volume because ETF purchases happen off-chain.

### Pattern VOL-4: Thin Liquidity Hours Exploitation

- **Description:** Crypto order book depth varies dramatically by time of day. Binance BTC/USDT shows $3.86 million in depth within 10bps at 11:00 UTC, but only $2.71 million at 21:00 UTC (a 42% reduction). The thinnest period is 02:00-06:00 UTC (between Asia closing and Europe opening).
- **Historical Win Rate:** Stop hunts and large moves disproportionately occur during thin-liquidity windows.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Weekend and holiday trading sessions regularly feature 30-50% reduction in order book depth.
  - The October 2025 flash crash ($3.21B liquidated in one minute) began during a lower-liquidity window.
- **When It Fails:** As institutional 24/7 desks expand (dedicated crypto trading firms, market makers), the liquidity differential between sessions is gradually narrowing.

### Pattern VOL-5: Order Book Imbalance as Micro-Signal

- **Description:** Order Book Imbalance (OBI) measures the ratio of bid vs. ask volume near the current price. OBI > +0.3 = heavy buying pressure (bullish). OBI < -0.3 = heavy selling pressure (bearish). This predicts price movement on a seconds-to-minutes timeframe.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Nearly linear relationship between OBI and short-horizon price changes documented in academic research. Predictive window typically lasts seconds to ~1 minute.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Sustained buying imbalance above +0.3 combined with increasing bid depth signals near-term price increase.
  - Selling imbalance below -0.3 with growing ask-side pressure signals impending decline.
- **When It Fails:** Spoofing creates false imbalances. Market makers can place fake orders to distort the OBI signal. A visible imbalance may be a trap.

### Pattern VOL-6: Spoofing and Ghost Wall Detection

- **Description:** Spoofing accounted for 37% of all detected market manipulation cases across major crypto exchanges (CME Group 2022 report). "Ghost walls" appear as large liquidity zones that are visually persistent in heatmaps but disappear as price approaches. They are placed to create false impressions of support/resistance.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Identifying and fading ghost walls is profitable for experienced traders with proper tooling.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Large orders (e.g., 5,000 BTC) placed on the bid side just below price, then canceled milliseconds before execution.
  - Order book heatmaps reveal these patterns over time.
- **When It Fails:** Sometimes a "ghost wall" is actually a real iceberg order that will absorb price -- the challenge is distinguishing spoofing from genuine hidden liquidity.

### Pattern VOL-7: Iceberg Order Detection

- **Description:** Iceberg orders are large orders broken into smaller visible chunks that auto-replenish. Detection: when a trade executes at higher volume than the displayed liquidity at the best price, and the displayed order refills within milliseconds.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Correctly identifying an iceberg signals genuine large-player interest. Trading in the direction of the iceberg is profitable ~65% of the time.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - A displayed order of 10 BTC that keeps refilling every time it's hit, while executions total 200+ BTC at that level, confirms an iceberg.
- **When It Fails:** "Order book stuffing" -- placing large visible orders specifically to mislead iceberg detection algorithms.

### Pattern VOL-8: Exchange Volume Divergence

- **Description:** When centralized exchange spot volume diverges significantly from on-chain volume, it signals wash trading or artificial activity. Genuine rallies should show correlated spot + on-chain volume increases.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~70% accuracy for identifying genuine vs. artificial volume spikes.
- **When It Fails:** Some legitimate trading activity (arbitrage, market-making) occurs purely on-exchange without on-chain settlements.

### Pattern VOL-9: Volume-Weighted Average Price (VWAP) Anchoring

- **Description:** Anchoring VWAP to major pivots (cycle lows, halving dates, ETF launch) creates invisible support/resistance levels that institutional traders respect. Price tends to gravitate toward anchored VWAP from significant dates.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~60% as support/resistance on daily timeframes.
- **When It Fails:** In strongly trending markets, VWAP anchors can be left behind and become irrelevant until a major retracement.

### Pattern VOL-10: Derivatives Volume vs. Spot Volume Divergence

- **Description:** When derivatives (futures + options) volume exceeds spot volume by more than 10x, the market is driven by leverage rather than genuine buying/selling. This leverage-heavy regime is fragile and prone to liquidation cascades.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Extreme derivatives/spot ratios preceded the major crashes of November 2025 and October 2025.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - October 2025: Derivatives-to-spot ratio was extremely elevated before the $9.89 billion liquidation cascade.
- **When It Fails:** In the ETF era, spot volume through regulated channels may not be captured in standard crypto data feeds, making the ratio artificially elevated.

### Pattern VOL-11: Stablecoin Exchange Inflows as Buying Signal

- **Description:** Large stablecoin inflows to exchanges signal preparation to buy crypto. Large stablecoin withdrawals from exchanges signal profit-taking or risk-off positioning. USDT and USDC combined represent 90%+ of stablecoin market ($310+ billion total).
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~70% correlation between large stablecoin inflows and subsequent price appreciation within 1-7 days.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Rising stablecoin exchange reserves reliably preceded rallies in 2023 and 2024.
  - Falling stablecoin dominance (ratio of stables to total market cap) signals rotation into risk assets.
- **When It Fails:** Large stablecoin inflows can be for lending, derivatives collateral, or OTC settlement rather than spot purchasing.

### Pattern VOL-12: New Stablecoin Minting as Leading Indicator

- **Description:** Large minting events (creation of new USDT or USDC) often precede rallies by 1-3 days, as freshly minted stables represent new capital entering the ecosystem.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~65% for large minting events (>$500M USDT) preceding price appreciation.
- **When It Fails:** Stablecoins can be minted for non-crypto purposes (remittances, payments, settlement) that don't create buying pressure on BTC.

### Pattern VOL-13: Volume Profile -- Point of Control (POC)

- **Description:** The price level with the highest traded volume over a given period (the POC) acts as a magnet. Price tends to gravitate toward it during consolidation. A breakout away from the POC with volume is more significant.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~60% as a mean-reversion target during range-bound markets.
- **When It Fails:** In trending markets, the POC shifts quickly and previous levels become irrelevant.

### Pattern VOL-14: Options OI by Strike -- Gravitational Levels

- **Description:** Large clusters of options open interest at specific strike prices create "gravitational" levels due to dealer hedging. Track the strikes with highest OI as likely support/resistance zones, especially approaching expiry.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~60% as near-term support/resistance near expiry.
- **When It Fails:** When large directional flows overwhelm hedging activity, particularly around major news events.

### Pattern VOL-15: Perpetual vs. Spot Premium/Discount

- **Description:** When perpetual futures trade at a consistent premium to spot (positive basis), it signals bullish sentiment. When they trade at a discount, it signals bearish sentiment. The basis and the funding rate are related but distinct measures.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Extreme premiums (>1%) have preceded corrections ~65% of the time. Extreme discounts (<-0.5%) have preceded rallies ~70% of the time.
- **When It Fails:** Cash-and-carry arbitrage can keep the basis in check regardless of sentiment.

### Pattern VOL-16: Bid-Ask Spread as Volatility Precursor

- **Description:** Widening bid-ask spreads signal that market makers are pulling liquidity ahead of expected volatility. A sudden widening of spreads, even before price movement, is an early warning of incoming volatility.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Spread widening precedes major moves ~70% of the time by 30 seconds to 5 minutes.
- **When It Fails:** During normal daily sessions, spreads can widen briefly due to technical issues or single market maker withdrawal without predicting directional moves.

### Pattern VOL-17: Relative Volume Breakout Confirmation

- **Description:** Volume relative to the 20-period average (RVOL) above 2.0 during a breakout confirms genuine participation. RVOL below 0.5 during a "breakout" suggests it's likely a fakeout.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Breakouts with RVOL >2.0 sustain 72% of the time vs. 35% for RVOL <1.0.
- **When It Fails:** Flash crashes and algorithmic-driven moves can show extremely high RVOL that resolves quickly without sustained directional follow-through.

### Pattern VOL-18: Volume Exhaustion at Resistance

- **Description:** When price approaches a major resistance level with decreasing volume on each test, the resistance is likely to hold. Conversely, increasing volume on each test suggests the resistance will eventually break.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~65% accuracy for predicting resistance outcomes.
- **When It Fails:** A single large buyer or ETF flow can break resistance regardless of volume pattern.

### Pattern VOL-19: OTC Volume Signals

- **Description:** Large wallet-to-wallet transfers without exchange involvement indicate institutional OTC transactions with minimal market impact. These are generally bullish signals (institutions accumulating without wanting to move the market).
- **Historical Win Rate:** Sustained OTC accumulation patterns have preceded rallies in 2023-2024.
- **When It Fails:** OTC transactions can also be distributions (selling to buyers privately) that aren't visible in the data until the buyer's lockup expires.

### Pattern VOL-20: Taker Buy/Sell Ratio

- **Description:** The Taker Buy Sell Ratio measures the ratio of market buy orders to market sell orders. Values consistently above 1.0 signal aggressive buying; below 1.0 signals aggressive selling. Sustained periods above 1.05 or below 0.95 are directionally significant.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~60% as a short-term directional indicator (hours to days).
- **When It Fails:** Sophisticated traders use limit orders rather than market orders, making the taker ratio an incomplete picture of actual buying/selling pressure.

# 4. Correlation Patterns

### Pattern CORR-1: BTC-Equity Correlation Regime (Post-ETF)

- **Description:** Since Bitcoin ETF approval in January 2024, BTC-S&P 500 correlation has jumped from near-zero (historical average 0.13) to sustained levels around 0.5, with BTC-Nasdaq correlations reaching 0.87 after MicroStrategy's Nasdaq 100 inclusion. BTC now behaves as leveraged beta on risk assets.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Correlation holds during "risk-on" periods and intensifies during "risk-off" periods.
- **Specific Examples:**

  - 2023: S&P 500 +26%, BTC +147%.
  - Post-ETF BTC-Nasdaq 100 correlation peaked at 0.87.
- **When It Fails:** Crypto-specific events override equity correlation. The March 2023 banking crisis saw BTC rally 20.5% while S&P 500 rose only 5% (BTC as "anti-bank" safe haven). The FTX collapse in 2022 caused BTC to fall while equities were stable.

### Pattern CORR-2: When BTC-Equity Correlation Breaks (Contrarian Signal)

- **Description:** Extended periods of negative BTC-Nasdaq correlation mirror past periods (July 2021, September 2023, August 2024) that aligned with significant bitcoin lows. When BTC decouples negatively from equities, it has historically been a buying opportunity.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Negative correlation periods have preceded BTC recoveries approximately 75% of the time.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - December 2025: BTC's negative correlation with Nasdaq persisted, and history suggested a bottom was forming.
- **When It Fails:** If the negative correlation is driven by a crypto-specific existential threat (exchange collapse, regulatory ban), the equity market won't provide support.

### Pattern CORR-3: DXY Inverse Correlation

- **Description:** BTC and the US Dollar Index (DXY) often move inversely. Over the past five years, BTC-DXY correlation has fluctuated between -0.4 and -0.8. The Q1 2024 correlation was -0.65.
- **Historical Win Rate:** The inverse relationship holds during major macro shifts but is weaker than traditional assets' dollar correlation.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - A strengthening dollar tightens global financial conditions, constraining demand for speculative assets including BTC.
  - When DXY fell sharply in late 2023, BTC rallied from $25,000 to $45,000.
- **When It Fails:** Crypto-specific factors can overshadow dollar trends. BTC fell in late 2022 despite dollar weakness due to the FTX collapse. Similar breakdowns occurred around Coinbase's Nasdaq debut and the launch of the futures-based ETF.

### Pattern CORR-4: Gold-BTC Correlation During Risk Events

- **Description:** BTC and gold correlation is episodic, not inherent. During the 2023 SVB banking crisis, BTC and gold exhibited strong positive correlation (both rising as safe havens). In normal markets, their correlation is low. In the Trump 2.0 era (2025), the BTC-gold correlation broke down.
- **Historical Win Rate:** BTC acts as a "digital gold" during banking/financial crises but not during geopolitical crises (where gold outperforms).
- **Specific Examples:**
  - SVB crisis (March 2023): BTC and gold both rallied.
  - Ukraine conflict (2022): Gold rallied; BTC initially fell.
  - Q1 2025: Gold and Bitcoin moved in tight correlation from 2022-2024, but that relationship broke early 2025.
- **When It Fails:** BTC cannot be relied upon as a crisis hedge. Its correlation with risk-on assets and susceptibility to leverage-driven crashes make it an unreliable safe haven compared to gold.

### Pattern CORR-5: DXY Reversal as BTC Buy Signal

- **Description:** Major DXY trend reversals (from strengthening to weakening) have historically been powerful BTC buy signals. A confirmed DXY death cross (50-day below 200-day) on the DXY chart is bullish for BTC.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~70% correlation between DXY downtrends and BTC uptrends.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Late 2022 to 2023: DXY peaked and began declining; BTC bottomed and began rallying.
- **When It Fails:** Short-term DXY fluctuations don't reliably predict BTC moves. The signal works best on macro timeframes (months, not days).

### Pattern CORR-6: Cross-Crypto Correlation Breakdown as Cycle Signal

- **Description:** When BTC completely decouples from altcoins (rising while alts stagnate), it can signal excess upward movement and warn of potential bull market end within 12 months. Post-Bitcoin-ETF, institutional capital entered BTC without proportional altcoin investment, creating structural decoupling.
- **Historical Win Rate:** BTC-altcoin decoupling has preceded cycle tops in 2017 and 2021.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Late 2017: BTC surged to $20,000 while many alts were flat, then everything crashed.
  - 2024-2025: Pronounced post-ETF decline in BTC-alt correlations.
- **When It Fails:** The 2024 ETF-driven decoupling may be structural rather than cyclical, meaning the traditional "BTC leads, then alts follow" pattern may not apply as strongly.

### Pattern CORR-7: Stablecoin Supply as Crypto Liquidity Proxy

- **Description:** Total stablecoin supply exceeds $310 billion and serves as a proxy for crypto market liquidity. Rising stablecoin supply (fresh capital entering) = bullish for crypto markets. Declining stablecoin supply = bearish (capital leaving).
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~75% correlation between stablecoin supply growth rate and BTC price direction.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - USDT market cap above $183 billion, USDC above $75 billion (combined 90%+ share).
  - Stablecoin supply contraction in 2022 (post-LUNA collapse) preceded further crypto declines.
  - Stablecoin supply expansion throughout 2023-2024 preceded the BTC rally.
- **When It Fails:** Stablecoin supply growth can reflect non-crypto demand (payments, remittances, institutional settlement) rather than crypto buying intent.

### Pattern CORR-8: US Rate Cycle and BTC

- **Description:** BTC is positively correlated with liquidity conditions. Rate cuts (easing) are bullish; rate hikes (tightening) are bearish. But BTC front-runs rate decisions -- it rallies on expectations of cuts and sells on the actual event ("buy the rumor, sell the news").
- **Historical Win Rate:** BTC's sensitivity to Fed policy has increased dramatically since institutional adoption.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - 2022: Aggressive rate hikes drove BTC from $47,000 to $15,500.
  - Late 2023: Anticipation of rate cuts drove BTC from $25,000 to $44,000 before any cuts materialized.
- **When It Fails:** Crypto-specific catalysts (ETF approval, halving) can overpower macro rate dynamics.

### Pattern CORR-9: Yield Curve and Crypto Risk Appetite

- **Description:** An inverted yield curve (short rates > long rates) suppresses risk appetite, including crypto. Yield curve steepening (normalization) tends to coincide with increased risk appetite and crypto rallies.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~65% directional correlation.
- **When It Fails:** Crypto's unique supply dynamics (halvings, ETF flows) can override yield curve signals.

### Pattern CORR-10: Chinese Yuan Weakness and BTC

- **Description:** Historical correlation between CNY weakness and BTC strength, as capital flight from China finds crypto as a conduit. This was more relevant pre-2021 when Chinese exchanges dominated, but the dynamic still exists through OTC channels.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Diminished since China's 2021 crypto ban but still observable during acute CNY weakness.
- **When It Fails:** Chinese capital controls have become more effective, reducing the magnitude of this flow.

### Pattern CORR-11: Oil Price Shocks and BTC

- **Description:** Major oil price spikes create stagflation fears that are bearish for all risk assets including BTC. However, oil price crashes (deflation signal) can be initially bearish (risk-off) but then bullish if they lead to rate cuts.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - March 2026: Oil shock combined with recession fears created oversold BTC conditions.
- **When It Fails:** The indirect nature of the oil-BTC relationship makes timing unreliable.

### Pattern CORR-12: US Election Cycle and BTC

- **Description:** BTC has shown positive performance in U.S. election years, particularly in Q4. The "election year effect" in crypto is amplified by policy uncertainty and candidates' stances on regulation.
- **Historical Win Rate:** BTC has risen in 3/3 recent election years (2016, 2020, 2024).
- **Specific Examples:**
  - 2024: BTC rallied from $42,000 to $100,000+ in the election year, with Trump's pro-crypto stance as a catalyst.
  - 2020: BTC rallied from $7,000 to $29,000 in the election year.
- **When It Fails:** If a strongly anti-crypto candidate wins, the election year pattern could reverse.

### Pattern CORR-13: Global M2 Money Supply and BTC

- **Description:** BTC has a strong long-term correlation with global M2 money supply growth. Periods of M2 expansion correspond to BTC rallies; M2 contraction corresponds to BTC declines. The lag is approximately 2-3 months.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~80% directional accuracy on quarterly timeframes.
- **When It Fails:** Short-term crypto-specific events can override the M2 signal.

### Pattern CORR-14: Tech Stock Earnings and BTC Volatility

- **Description:** Major tech earnings (AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL, META, AMZN) create risk-on/risk-off events that spill into crypto within hours. Positive earnings broadly = bullish for BTC; negative = bearish.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Increased since BTC-Nasdaq correlation rose post-ETF.
- **When It Fails:** When crypto is in its own narrative-driven cycle, tech earnings are noise.

### Pattern CORR-15: Bitcoin-Stablecoin Divergence

- **Description:** When BTC price drops but stablecoin exchange deposits rise, it signals "dry powder" being positioned for buying. This divergence is one of the most reliable short-term bullish signals.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~75% accuracy for identifying near-term (1-2 week) bottoms.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - After the June 2022 crash, stablecoin exchange deposits surged even as BTC fell, signaling incoming demand.
- **When It Fails:** If the stablecoin deposits are used for yield farming or transferred to other venues rather than spot buying.

# 5. Time-Based Patterns

### Pattern TIME-1: Asian Session Range-Setting (00:00-09:00 UTC)

- **Description:** The Asian session (Japan, Korea, Singapore) enters at midnight UTC. Trading is generally calmer with lower volatility, and the market often remains in a range. However, lower liquidity can create unexpected moves -- smaller orders can move the market more significantly, leading to stop-loss hunting or unexpected breakouts.
- **Historical Win Rate:** The Asian session range frequently defines the day's initial support/resistance levels.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Asian sessions often see retail-driven moves that are later reversed by European or US institutional flows.
- **When It Fails:** Major Asian regulatory news (China bans, Japan regulations, Korean exchange issues) can create directional Asian sessions that carry into European hours.

### Pattern TIME-2: European Session Trend-Setting (07:00-16:00 UTC)

- **Description:** London and Frankfurt open around 7-8 AM UTC, and from this moment the market becomes more active with volumes increasing. The European open forms the "basis of the day" -- the directional bias established during European hours tends to persist.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~55% chance that the direction established in the first 2 hours of European trading persists for the day.
- **When It Fails:** When the US session brings a contradictory catalyst (FOMC decision, US data release, ETF flow data).

### Pattern TIME-3: US Session Dominance (13:30-22:00 UTC)

- **Description:** The US session is the most powerful flow of capital. Main trends of the day are formed here, and volatility reaches its maximum. The US session is the most volatile due to overlap with both European and Asian markets.
- **Historical Win Rate:** The US session accounts for the majority of daily BTC volume and the largest average price moves.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - ETF-related flows create a pronounced US session effect since January 2024.
  - The "10 AM dump" pattern (Pattern BTC-32) is a US session-specific phenomenon.
- **When It Fails:** On US holidays, volume drops dramatically and the session effect disappears.

### Pattern TIME-4: EU-US Overlap -- Peak Volatility Window (12:00-16:00 UTC)

- **Description:** The overlap between European and US sessions (12:00-16:00 UTC) is the most volatile period of the trading day. This is when the most significant directional moves typically occur.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Consistently the highest volume and volatility window.
- **When It Fails:** During quiet macro periods with no catalyst, even the overlap window can be subdued.

### Pattern TIME-5: The Dead Zone (02:00-06:00 UTC)

- **Description:** Between Asia heading into night and Europe not yet waking up (02:00-06:00 UTC), the market "almost falls asleep." However, this is precisely when stop-hunting and manipulation are most effective due to thin order books.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Flash crashes and wicks disproportionately occur during the dead zone.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Binance BTC depth drops 42% from peak hours (11:00 UTC) to low hours (21:00 UTC), with even further reduction during the dead zone.
- **When It Fails:** During major global news events that break outside normal market hours.

### Pattern TIME-6: "Sell in May and Go Away" -- Crypto Version

- **Description:** The traditional equity pattern has mixed applicability to crypto. May has actually shown above-average returns of 22.1% since 2011 for BTC. The weak period is June-September (Q3 averages only 6% return vs. Q4's 85.4%).
- **Historical Win Rate:** "Sell in May" is NOT reliable for crypto. The weak period is more accurately June-September.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Q2 average return: 26%. Q3 average return: 6%. Q4 average return: 85.4%.
  - April, May, October, and November have historically been the best months.
  - June through September have shown the weakest performances.
- **When It Fails:** Cycle-specific events (halving in April 2024, ETF launches) can override seasonal patterns entirely.

### Pattern TIME-7: September -- The Worst Month ("Septembear")

- **Description:** Since 2013, Bitcoin's average monthly return in September has been -4.56%, making it by far Bitcoin's worst month. Bitcoin has traded down in 9 of 13 Septembers on record. Losses range from 1.76% to 19.01%.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~69% chance of negative September (9/13 years).
- **Specific Examples:**
  - The September effect is widely recognized and may be partly self-fulfilling.
- **When It Fails:** September 2023 broke the pattern (+3.9%) during the pre-ETF anticipation rally.

### Pattern TIME-8: "Uptober" -- The Best Month

- **Description:** October is nicknamed "Uptober" thanks to Bitcoin's approximately 22-30% average rise during the month. Nine of the past eleven Octobers ended positively.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~82% chance of positive October (9/11 years).
- **Specific Examples:**
  - October average return: 22.90% (some calculations show 29.88%).
  - October 2024: Strong rally accompanying ETF inflows.
- **When It Fails:** October 2018 was flat (-4%) during the depths of the bear market.

### Pattern TIME-9: November -- Historically Strongest

- **Description:** November has the highest average monthly return at 37.51%, making it BTC's historically strongest month in absolute terms.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~70% chance of positive November.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - November 2013: BTC rallied from ~$200 to $1,100 (+450%).
  - November 2020: BTC rallied from $13,500 to $19,000 (+41%).
  - November 2024: BTC broke $100,000 for the first time.
- **When It Fails:** November 2022 saw the FTX collapse and a major crash (-16%). Crypto-specific black swans override seasonality.

### Pattern TIME-10: Q4 Dominance

- **Description:** Q4 (October-December) is historically the strongest quarter for Bitcoin with an average return of 85.4%. This is when institutional year-end positioning, tax strategies, and narratives converge.
- **Historical Win Rate:** ~75% chance of positive Q4.
- **When It Fails:** Bear market Q4s (2018, 2022) can be devastating.

### Pattern TIME-11: January Effect -- Post-Tax-Loss-Harvesting Rebound

- **Description:** December tax-loss harvesting (selling underwater crypto to offset gains) creates concentrated selling pressure in the last days of the year. In the US, crypto lacks a wash-sale rule, meaning investors can sell and immediately rebuy. This "harvest and rebuy" activity fuels coordinated buybacks in early January, creating a rebound.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Bitcoin price rebounds at the start of each year since 2023.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - December 2025: Bitcoin's 30% slide from ATH spurred record tax-loss harvesting activity. Selling was concentrated in the final days of the year.
  - The lack of wash-sale constraints makes the "harvest-and-rebuy" trade easy to execute immediately.
- **When It Fails:** If December selling reflects genuine capitulation rather than tax optimization, January may not see a rebound (e.g., January 2022 continued the decline from November 2021 ATH).

### Pattern TIME-12: Mid-Month Weakness

- **Description:** Research on Bitcoin ETPs shows that the middle portion of the month tends to generate negative returns, while the last trading days and first days of the new month are stronger.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Observable pattern but with limited sample size.
- **When It Fails:** Monthly patterns are the weakest of all time-based patterns due to small sample sizes and high variance.

### Pattern TIME-13: Post-Conference Dump

- **Description:** Major crypto conferences (Bitcoin Miami, Consensus, Token2049) tend to precede price weakness. The "conference effect" is a "buy the rumor, sell the news" dynamic where participants sell after the event.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Observable but variable.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - Bitcoin Conference 2022 (Miami): 29% drawdown in the month following. However, this was during a bear market.
  - The pattern is most pronounced during bear markets and neutral markets, not during strong bull markets.
- **When It Fails:** During strong bull markets, conferences can actually fuel FOMO and accelerate rallies. The November 2024 bull run wasn't dampened by conferences.

### Pattern TIME-14: FOMC Meeting Volatility Windows

- **Description:** Federal Reserve meetings create predictable volatility windows in crypto. The pattern: compression 24-48 hours before the announcement -> spike on the announcement -> follow-through or reversal within 24 hours. BTC's sensitivity to Fed policy has increased dramatically since institutional adoption.
- **Historical Win Rate:** FOMC meetings produce 3-5% BTC moves ~70% of the time.
- **When It Fails:** When the FOMC outcome is perfectly priced in (no surprise), volatility can be lower than expected.

### Pattern TIME-15: CPI Release Day Pattern

- **Description:** US CPI releases (typically second week of the month) create the largest single-day crypto volatility events outside of crypto-specific catalysts. Lower-than-expected CPI = bullish (rate cut expectations). Higher-than-expected CPI = bearish.
- **Historical Win Rate:** CPI releases move BTC 3-7% in the hours following release approximately 60% of the time.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - January 2023 CPI: Lower than expected, BTC rallied 5% within hours.
  - September 2022 CPI: Higher than expected, BTC dropped 4% within hours.
- **When It Fails:** When crypto is in a strong narrative cycle, CPI impact is muted (e.g., during the March 2024 ETF-driven rally, CPI prints had diminished impact).

### Pattern TIME-16: Halving Year Seasonality

- **Description:** In halving years, the typical Q3 weakness is even more pronounced (post-halving consolidation), but Q4 performance is amplified. The halving year template: Q1 rally (anticipation), Q2 flat/dip (sell the news), Q3 weakness (consolidation), Q4 breakout (supply reduction kicks in).
- **Historical Win Rate:** This template held in 2016 and 2020. 2024 partially followed it.
- **When It Fails:** The 2024 cycle front-ran the halving so aggressively (ATH in March) that the template was distorted.

### Pattern TIME-17: Options Expiry Calendar Rhythm

- **Description:** Predictable volatility windows around options expiry: weekly (Friday 8:00 UTC Deribit), monthly (last Friday of month), quarterly (last Friday of March/June/September/December). Quarterly expirations ($5B+) produce the most significant post-expiry moves.
- **Historical Win Rate:** Quarterly expirations are the most reliable volatility catalysts.
- **Specific Examples:**
  - December 2025: $27 billion in year-end options expiry created significant positioning.
- **When It Fails:** When expiry notional is small or widely distributed across strikes.

# Appendix: Pattern Interaction Rules

## Rule 1: Signal Confluence Multiplier
When 3+ patterns from different categories align (e.g., on-chain + derivatives + time-based all bearish), the probability of the predicted move increases significantly. Never trade a single pattern in isolation.

## Rule 2: Regime Classification First
Before applying any pattern, classify the current regime:
- **Bull Trend:** Bullish patterns have higher win rates; bearish patterns should be weighted down.
- **Bear Trend:** Bearish patterns have higher win rates; bullish patterns should be weighted down.
- **Range/Consolidation:** Mean-reversion patterns (funding rate, OBI, volume divergences) have the highest win rates.

## Rule 3: Timeframe Hierarchy
Higher timeframe signals override lower timeframe signals. A weekly bearish divergence trumps a daily bullish setup. Always check the weekly chart before acting on a 4-hour signal.

## Rule 4: Liquidity Conditions Override
In thin liquidity environments (weekends, holidays, dead zones), ALL pattern reliability decreases. Reduce position sizes and widen stops.

## Rule 5: Post-ETF Regime Shift
Since January 2024, many historical crypto-only patterns have been modified by institutional flows. Patterns that rely on retail behavior (weekend effects, exchange-specific moves) are less reliable. Patterns tied to institutional behavior (ETF flows, quarter-end rebalancing, equity correlation) are more reliable.

## Rule 6: The Anti-Crowding Principle
When a pattern becomes widely known and discussed on crypto Twitter/social media, its effectiveness decreases. The most crowded trades (e.g., "Uptober" in 2025, CME gap fills) can fail spectacularly precisely because everyone is positioned for them.

## Rule 7: On-Chain Confirmation
For any macro signal (MVRV, NUPL, exchange reserves), always seek confirmation from at least one real-time signal (funding rates, OI, order flow) before acting. On-chain metrics are slower-moving and can give premature signals.
