# SECTION 1: MAJOR CRYPTO MARKET CYCLES

## Cycle 1: 2013-2014 -- The First Bubble

**Bull Phase:**
- BTC started 2013 at ~$13, surged to $266 in April (Cyprus banking crisis catalyst), corrected, then ripped to $1,163 by December 2013.
- Full-year 2013 return: ~9,200%.
- The rally was driven by early-adopter enthusiasm, Cyprus bail-in fears (first "store of value" narrative), and thin liquidity amplifying moves.

**Bear Phase (2014-2015):**
- Mt. Gox, handling ~70% of all BTC transactions globally, halted withdrawals on Feb 7, 2014. Filed bankruptcy Feb 28, 2014.
- 850,000 BTC lost (~7% of all Bitcoin in existence). Later recovered 200,000 BTC from an old wallet.
- BTC crashed from $1,163 to $152 -- an **87% drawdown** over ~14 months.
- Bottom was reached in January 2015 at approximately $152-$170.
- Bear market lasted roughly 410 days from peak to trough.

**Key Lesson:** Counterparty risk is the #1 killer. The largest exchange failing wiped out the market for over a year.

## Cycle 2: 2017-2018 -- The ICO Boom and Crash

**Bull Phase (2017):**
- BTC started 2017 at ~$1,000. Reached $19,783 on Dec 17, 2017. Gain: ~1,900%.
- ETH went from $8 (Jan 2017) to $1,400 (Jan 2018) -- a 17,400% gain.
- Total crypto market cap went from ~$18B to ~$830B.
- ICOs raised $5.6B in 2017 alone, $12B more in 2018. Total ~$20B raised between early 2017 and Oct 2018.
- Largest ICOs: EOS ($4B), Tezos ($232M), Filecoin ($257M).
- 64% of 5,384 ICOs launched between 2015-2020 failed outright.

**Bear Phase (2018-2019):**
- BTC peaked at $19,783 on Dec 17, 2017. Bottomed at $3,122 on Dec 15, 2018. **Drawdown: 84%.**
- ETH peaked at ~$1,400 in January 2018. Bottomed at ~$80 in December 2018. **Drawdown: 94%.**
- Total crypto market cap fell from $830B to ~$100B.
- Bear market duration from peak to trough: ~365 days.
- Catalysts for the decline: Regulatory crackdowns on ICOs, South Korea ban fears (Jan 12, 2018), Coincheck hack ($530M NEM stolen, Jan 26, 2018), rising Fed interest rates.
- SEC enforcement: Block.one (EOS) fined $24M. Telegram forced to return $1.2B and pay $18.5M fine. Telegram abandoned its TON project entirely.

**Key Lesson:** When the primary use case of a chain (Ethereum for ICOs) collapses, the chain asset crashes even harder than BTC. Regulatory risk was underpriced.

- This was the proof-of-concept moment for DeFi as a sector.

**Key Lesson:** Extreme fear (F&G = 9) during an exogenous shock (not a crypto-native failure) is the highest-conviction buy signal in crypto history. BTC rallied 1,800% over the next 20 months.

## Cycle 5: 2022 Bear Market -- The Great Unwind

**Luna/UST Collapse (May 2022):**
- UST (algorithmic stablecoin) was backed by LUNA and offered 19.5% yield on Anchor Protocol. Daily subsidies reached $6M by April 2022.
- May 7: Two large addresses withdrew 375M UST from Anchor. $2B UST removed total.
- May 9: UST depegged to $0.91. LUNA was at $62.
- May 13: LUNA at $0.0003. **Drawdown: 99.99%.** ~$50B in value destroyed.
- The "death spiral" mechanism: UST depeg --> LUNA minted to restore peg --> hyperinflation of LUNA supply --> further selling --> deeper UST depeg.
- Warning signs: Unsustainable 19.5% yield, growing subsidy costs ($6M/day), LUNA market cap declining relative to UST market cap (undercollateralization), large on-chain withdrawals visible to all participants.

**Three Arrows Capital (3AC) Collapse (June 2022):**
- 3AC managed ~$10B AUM as recently as March 2022. Had $200M in LUNA.
- Failed to meet margin calls from BlockFi and Genesis by mid-June.
- Ordered to liquidate June 27, 2022. Remaining assets: ~$400M against $2B+ debts.
- Contagion: Voyager Digital bankruptcy ($670M exposure). Blockchain.com $270M loss. Genesis, BlockFi, BitMEX all hit.
- Total crypto market cap fell 48% in May-June 2022 alone.

**FTX Collapse (November 2022):**
- Nov 2: CoinDesk revealed Alameda Research's balance sheet was dominated by FTT (FTX's own token).
- Nov 6: Binance CEO CZ announced selling all FTT holdings. $1B withdrawn from FTX in one day.
- Nov 7: $4B more in withdrawals. FTX liquidity crisis.
- Nov 8: Withdrawals frozen. Binance acquisition proposed then abandoned within 24 hours after due diligence.
- Nov 11: FTX + 100 entities filed bankruptcy. $8B hole in customer accounts discovered.
- BTC fell from ~$21,000 to $15,476 (Nov 2022 low). **Cycle drawdown from $68,789 ATH: 77.5%.**
- SOL crashed from ~$35 to $8 (96% from ATH) due to FTX/Alameda heavy SOL holdings.

## The Rotation Sequence

**Confirmed historical pattern:**
1. **BTC leads** -- dominance rises as smart money accumulates BTC first (lowest risk, highest liquidity).
2. **BTC dominance peaks** (typically 60-70%) -- BTC rallies slow, profits rotate.
3. **ETH catches up** -- ETH/BTC ratio rises. ETH/BTC weekly close above 0.058 has preceded every major alt season since 2017.
4. **Large-cap L1 alts rally** (SOL, AVAX, etc.) -- BTC dominance drops through 55%.
5. **Mid-caps and DeFi tokens** surge -- BTC.D drops through 50%.
6. **Small-caps, meme coins, NFTs** go parabolic -- BTC.D approaching 40%. THIS IS THE TOP SIGNAL.

**Timing:** The full rotation from BTC-leading to meme-coin-mania typically takes 6-12 months. Alt season itself (BTC.D declining) lasts 2-4 months in its most intense phase.

# SECTION 6: MAJOR BLACK SWAN EVENTS AND WARNING SIGNS

## 1. Mt. Gox Hack (Feb 2014)
- **What:** World's largest exchange lost 850,000 BTC (7% of all BTC). Handled 70% of global BTC volume.
- **Price Impact:** BTC fell from $828 to $440 (-47%) in February-March 2014. Eventually bottomed at $152 (-87% from cycle high).
- **Warning Signs:** Withdrawal delays began weeks before collapse. Reports of "transaction malleability" issues used as cover. Massive insolvency hidden from public.
- **Lesson:** If an exchange freezes withdrawals, get out immediately via any available means.

## 2. China Ban Cycles

| Year | Action | Immediate BTC Impact | 6-Month Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dec 2013 | PBOC bans banks from BTC | -13% immediate, then $1K --> $400 | Bear market |
| Sep 2017 | ICO ban + exchange shutdown | $5,000 --> $3,000 (-40%) | Recovered to $19K ATH |
| May 2021 | Mining ban (65% of global hashrate) | $43K --> $29K (-33%) | Recovered to $69K ATH |

**Pattern:** Each successive China ban has LESS lasting impact. The market has become desensitized. The 2021 mining ban -- the most severe action ever -- was fully recovered within 5 months.

## 3. Luna/UST Depeg (May 2022)
- **Warning Signs (Visible in Advance):**
  - 19.5% Anchor yield was mathematically unsustainable. Daily subsidy costs reached $6M.
  - LUNA market cap was declining relative to outstanding UST supply -- the collateral ratio was deteriorating.
  - On May 1, 2022, community voted to reduce Anchor yield (signal that insiders knew it was unsustainable).
  - May 7: Two whale addresses withdrew 375M UST from Anchor -- visible on-chain to anyone watching.
  - The total $2B UST withdrawal from Anchor was observable in real-time on blockchain explorers.
- **What Made It a Systemic Event:** The "death spiral" was a known theoretical risk. Academic papers had modeled it. Yet $50B was invested anyway because yield was attractive.
- **Tradeable Signal:** Large concentrated on-chain outflows from the yield protocol should have triggered an immediate risk-off posture.

## 4. FTX Collapse (Nov 2022)
- **Warning Signs (Visible in Advance):**
  - Nov 2: CoinDesk reported Alameda's balance sheet was heavily concentrated in FTT (FTX's own token) -- circular collateral.
  - FTT was illiquid relative to its notional value on Alameda's books.
  - FTX's proof of reserves was never independently verified.
  - Alameda CEO Caroline Ellison offered to buy CZ's FTT OTC at $22 -- desperation signal.
- **Speed of Collapse:** 9 days from CoinDesk article to bankruptcy filing.
- **Tradeable Signal:** The CoinDesk article on Nov 2 was the signal. Anyone who withdrew that day preserved capital. $1B left in one day (Nov 6), $4B more on Nov 7.
- **Lesson:** When an exchange's token is also the collateral for its own trading arm, it is a ponzi structure. Avoid any exchange where the native token comprises a significant portion of the balance sheet.

## 5. SVB / USDC Depeg (March 2023)
- **Timeline:**
  - March 9: SVB closed by California regulators.
  - March 10 (10pm): Circle disclosed $3.3B of USDC reserves stuck at SVB (8% of total reserves).
  - March 11 (3am): Panic selling. USDC dropped to $0.87 on Kraken.
  - Ethereum gas fees spiked to 231 gwei (from 20-40 normal).
  - March 12 (Sunday): Fed announced all SVB depositors would be made whole.
  - March 13: USDC recovered to $1.00.
- **Paradoxical Outcome:** BTC rallied 20.5% in March 2023 as money fled stablecoins INTO BTC as a "safe haven" from banking system risk. This was the first time BTC clearly decoupled from traditional risk-off behavior.
- **Tradeable Signal:** The depeg to $0.87 was a buying opportunity for USDC (if you trusted Circle's solvency + Fed backstop) and a buying opportunity for BTC (as capital fled banking exposure).

# SECTION 7: KEY METRICS AND WHAT THEY HISTORICALLY PREDICT

## RSI (Relative Strength Index)

**Oversold (RSI <30) Historical Instances:**
- January 2015: Weekly RSI ~28 near $200. Bottom. Consolidated 8 months, then sustained recovery.
- December 2018: Weekly RSI <30 near $3,200. Bottom. Consolidated ~3 months, then broke higher.
- February 2026: Weekly RSI hit 25.6 -- lowest since 2016.

**Overbought (RSI >70) Historical Instances:**
- RSI >90 on monthly chart occurred at every major cycle top (Dec 2013, Dec 2017, Nov 2021).
- RSI >80 without >90 has been a false alarm in mid-cycle corrections.

**Reliability:**
- RSI <30 on weekly = strong buy signal for 6-12 month horizon (~85% win rate historically).
- RSI >90 on monthly = high-confidence cycle top signal.
- RSI between 30-70 = noise. Not actionable alone.
- Caveat: In strong downtrends, RSI can stay oversold for extended periods ("can stay oversold longer than you can stay solvent").

## Volume Climax Patterns

- A volume spike 3-5x the 20-day average, combined with a long-wick candle (hammer at bottom / shooting star at top), has marked 75-85% of major reversals.
- The pattern is strongest when accompanied by RSI divergence.
- Head & Shoulders: 80% success rate when confirmed with 25-30% volume increase on breakdown.
- Inverse Head & Shoulders: 84% success rate historically in crypto -- the most reliable reversal pattern.
- Double/Triple Bottoms: 75-85% reliability with volume confirmation.

## Funding Rates (Perpetual Futures)

- **Extreme Positive Funding (>0.05%/8hr):** Long-heavy market. Cost of holding longs becomes punitive. Conditions ripe for long squeeze / liquidation cascade.
- **Extreme Negative Funding (<-0.03%/8hr):** Short-heavy market. Shorts are paying longs. Conditions ripe for short squeeze.
- **October 2025 Liquidation Cascade:** $964M in BTC positions liquidated in one day. $1.7-$2.0B across crypto. 396,000 traders liquidated. Open interest fell 35% from $94B peak.
- **Key Signal:** When funding rates are deeply positive AND open interest is at highs, the market is maximally fragile. A small sell order can cascade: $50M sell moved price hundreds of dollars when levered longs' bids evaporated.
- **Rule:** Extreme positive funding + high OI + declining spot volume = imminent correction (70%+ probability).

## Exchange Inflows/Outflows

**Inflows (Bearish):**
- Large net inflows = coins moving to exchanges for potential sale.
- Historically precedes price drops when sustained over multiple days.

**Outflows (Bullish):**
- Large net outflows = coins moving to cold storage/self-custody. Long-term holding behavior.
- Dec 29, 2023: 28,000 BTC ($1.19B) left exchanges in single day -- largest outflow since Dec 2022. BTC was at ~$42K. Rallied to $73K within 3 months.
- Since Dec 7, 2024: 403,000 BTC (2% of total supply) left exchanges.
- Exchange BTC balance at multi-year lows (~2.3M BTC, lowest since April 2018).

# SECTION 8: MACRO CORRELATIONS

## BTC vs S&P 500

**Long-Term Correlation:** 0.17 over 10 years. 0.41 over last 5 years. The correlation has structurally increased as institutional participation has grown.

**When Correlation is Highest:**
- During liquidity-driven risk-on/risk-off events (2020 COVID crash: everything correlated to 1.0).
- During Fed tightening cycles (2022: BTC and S&P moved together, but BTC with higher beta. S&P -18%, BTC -64%).
- Correlation peaked at 0.49 (90-day rolling) in March 2022.

**When Correlation Breaks:**
- **Crypto-native events:** FTX collapse (Nov 2022) -- BTC crashed, S&P was flat. ZERO equity correlation.
- **Banking crises:** SVB collapse (March 2023) -- BTC rallied 20.5% while banking stocks crashed. NEGATIVE correlation.
- **Supply-driven crypto rallies:** ETF approval, halving anticipation -- can rally independently of equities.

**Practical Rule:** Assume BTC and equities are correlated during macro-driven moves (Fed, liquidity). Assume decorrelation during crypto-specific catalysts. The more institutional BTC becomes, the more macro-correlated it gets in risk-off events.

## Fed Rate Decisions

**Historical Pattern:**
| Fed Regime | BTC Performance | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Rate cuts / QE (2020-2021) | Massive rallies ($8K --> $69K) | Cheap money floods into risk assets |
| Rate hikes (2022-2023) | Severe declines ($69K --> $15.5K) | Opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets rises; liquidity drains |
| Rate pause (2023-2024) | Recovery ($15.5K --> $73K) | Forward-looking: market prices in eventual cuts |
| Rate cuts begin (2024-2025) | Rally to new ATH ($126K) | Liquidity returns; risk appetite increases |

**Key Nuance:** Markets are forward-looking. The ANTICIPATION of rate changes moves crypto more than the actual decision. By the time the Fed cuts, the rally has often already priced it in. "Buy the rumor, sell the news" applies heavily.

**BTC vs Real Yields:** Bitcoin shows a strong inverse relationship with real yields (nominal yields minus inflation). When real yields are negative (rates below inflation), BTC thrives because holding cash has a guaranteed loss. When real yields are positive (2022: 10Y yield 4% with inflation falling), non-yielding assets like BTC face headwinds.

## DXY (Dollar Index) Correlation

**Core Relationship:** Inverse. DXY up = BTC down. DXY down = BTC up. This is the most consistent macro correlation in crypto.

**Why:**
1. Strong dollar tightens global liquidity (most debt is USD-denominated).
2. Emerging markets suffer when DXY rises, reducing risk appetite globally.
3. Weak dollar means accommodative monetary policy and excess liquidity seeking returns.

**Historical Confirmations:**
- DXY peaked at ~114 in September 2022. BTC was near its bear market low.
- DXY declined through 2023 toward ~100. BTC rallied from $16K to $44K.
- DXY peaks often coincide with BTC bottoms. DXY bottoms often coincide with BTC tops.

**Practical Rule:** Monitor DXY as a leading indicator. A sustained DXY breakdown below key support (e.g., 100) is one of the most bullish macro signals for BTC. A DXY breakout above 105+ is a headwind.

## Global Liquidity (M2)

- Both BTC and M2 money supply moved in the same direction in the second half of 2022 (both contracted).
- QE (money printing) is the ultimate fuel for crypto rallies. The 2020-2021 bull run was built on $4T+ in Fed stimulus.
- The correlation between BTC and global M2 with a ~3 month lag is one of the highest signal macro indicators available.
- **When M2 is expanding:** risk-on for all assets, especially BTC.
- **When M2 is contracting:** risk-off. BTC underperforms even equities.
