## 5. Optimal Position Sizing During Drawdowns

### Conservative Bear Market Position Sizing

| Parameter | Bear Market Setting |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Risk per trade | 0.5-0.75% (vs 1-2% in normal markets) |
| Stablecoin/cash allocation | 50-70% |
| Maximum single position | 1-3% of total capital |

### Dynamic Stablecoin Allocation by Bear Phase

| Market Phase | Stablecoin/Cash % | Rationale |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Early Bear (-30% from ATH) | 40% | Trend uncertain |
| Mid Bear (-50% from ATH) | 60% | Capital preservation priority |
| Late Bear (-70% from ATH) | 40% | Accumulation opportunity |
| Accumulation/Recovery | 20% | Increased conviction |

### Position Sizing Methods

1. **Kelly Criterion:** f = p - (1-p)/b (where p = win probability, b = reward/risk ratio)
   - Mathematically optimal but sensitive to input error
   - Use **fractional Kelly (0.25-0.5x)** in bear markets due to elevated uncertainty

2. **Volatility Parity / ATR-Based:** Size positions so each contributes similar volatility
   - Adapts automatically to changing conditions
   - Requires stable volatility estimates

3. **Fixed Fractional:** Robust for beginners; constant % risk per trade
   - Bear market adjustment: reduce from 1-2% to 0.5-0.75%

4. **Hybrid (Recommended):** Volatility-adjusted fixed fractional
   - Combines stability of fixed fractional with adaptiveness of volatility scaling
   - Position sizing scales with deviation magnitude -- larger deviations warrant larger positions (mean reversion probability increases)

## 7. Stop Loss Width: Tight vs Wide

### General Crypto Stop Loss Ranges

| Width | Range | Pros | Cons |
|-------|-------|------|------|
| Tight | 3-5% | Preserves capital, higher R:R ratio | Stopped out by noise; bear market whipsaws |
| Medium | 5-8% | Balance of protection and room | Standard approach |
| Wide | 8-10%+ | Higher win rate, fewer false stops | Larger per-trade losses |

### Bear Market Specific Evidence

- **Bear market short squeezes** can move 17%+ in 5 days (e.g., $63K to $74K)
- Tighter stops are **riskier in volatile bear markets** due to sudden reversals
- Below 3% stop: Essentially guaranteed stop-out due to normal crypto noise
- Above 10% stop: Exponentially harder to recover (a 10% loss requires 11.1% gain to break even; 20% loss requires 25%)

### ATR-Based Stop Loss (Volatility-Adaptive)

**Formula:**
- Long: Stop Loss = Entry Price - (ATR x Multiplier)
- Short: Stop Loss = Entry Price + (ATR x Multiplier)

**Optimal Multipliers:**

| Trading Style | ATR Period | Multiplier | Notes |
|--------------|-----------|------------|-------|
| Day trading | 7-10 | 1.5-2x | Fast reaction to volatility spikes |
| Swing trading | 14 | 2-2.5x | Standard for most strategies |
| Position trading | 21-30 | 2.5-3.5x | Larger trends |
| Crypto bear market | 7-14 | 2.5-3x+ | Higher multiplier for elevated volatility |

**Performance Data:**
- **2x ATR stop-loss reduced max drawdown by 32%** vs fixed stop-loss (study of 1,000 trades)
- **3x ATR multiplier boosted performance by 15%** vs fixed stop-loss methods
- Crypto often requires slightly higher ATR multiples due to extreme intraday moves

### Bear Market Recommendation

Use **ATR-based stops with 2.5-3x multiplier** during bear markets. Widen stops during volatility spikes to avoid unnecessary exits from temporary fluctuations. The key principle: stops should be placed outside the range of normal market noise, which is significantly wider in bear markets.

- Sharpe Ratio: **1.71**

- Smoothest returns across all market regimes

**Trend-Following (MAX strategy):**
- Remained effective during 2022-2024 out-of-sample period
- 20-day lookback outperformed 10-day during bear market
- Performed well when BTC was trending down consistently

**Mean Reversion (MIN strategy):**

### Key Bitcoin Behavior

- **At local maxima:** BTC tends to continue trending upward (momentum)
- **At local minima:** BTC tends to mean-revert and bounce back
- **Implication:** Use trend-following for entries in the direction of the macro trend; use mean-reversion for short-term tactical trades during panic selloffs

### Optimal Application in Bear Markets

1. **Primary strategy:** Trend following on the short side (ride the downtrend)
2. **Tactical overlay:** Mean reversion for catching oversold bounces (quick scalps)
3. **Combined approach:** Best risk-adjusted returns
4. **Bear-specific:** The velocity of bear markets creates more explosive trading days, making short-term mean reversion more effective

1. **Dynamic Algorithm Optimization:** Continuously adjust strategies in real-time based on market regime detection

## 14. Key Takeaways for CRO Implementation

### Bear Market Configuration Recommendations

Based on all historical evidence, the following parameters are recommended for a consensus-based trading system during bear markets:

#### Signal Quality

1. **Raise minimum consensus threshold to 75%+** (from typical 60-65% in bull markets)
2. **Require multi-timeframe confirmation** -- at least 2-3 timeframe alignment

3. **ATR-based sizing** to automatically adapt to volatility
4. **Maintain 50-70% stablecoin reserves**

1. **Use ATR-based stops with 2.5-3x multiplier** (wider than bull market 2x)

1. **Primary:** Trend-following on the short side
2. **Secondary:** Mean reversion for oversold bounces (quick scalps, tight targets)
