# Fundamental Manager Knowledge Base
# Rules for synthesizing valuation, supply dynamics, and cycle signals into actionable output.

---

## 1. Valuation vs Cycle Tension

### The Core Problem
A fundamentally cheap asset in a markdown (late bear) phase is a value trap, not an opportunity. Valuation without cycle context is dangerous.

### Decision Matrix

| Valuation | Cycle Phase | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Cheap | Accumulation (early) | BUY — Best risk/reward. Smart money is entering. |
| Cheap | Markdown (late bear) | WAIT — Valuation is correct but timing is wrong. Need cycle turn confirmation. |
| Cheap | Markup (bull) | BUY — Undervalued relative to peers. Rotation candidate. |
| Fair | Accumulation | HOLD/SMALL BUY — Not a bargain but cycle tailwind supports. |
| Fair | Markup | HOLD — Ride the trend. No urgency to add or reduce. |
| Fair | Distribution | REDUCE — Fair value in distribution = about to become expensive relative to post-correction price. |
| Expensive | Markup (mid-bull) | HOLD cautiously — Expensive in a bull can get more expensive. Do not short. Tighten stops. |
| Expensive | Distribution | SELL — The worst possible combination. Exit. |
| Expensive | Markdown | SHORT candidate — Expensive + falling. Full conviction bearish. |

### Cycle Confirmation Signals
- **Accumulation start**: Price forms a base (3+ months of range-bound action), volume declines to multi-month lows, then a volume surge on an up-move.
- **Markup start**: Price breaks above the accumulation range with volume. Higher highs and higher lows establish on the weekly.
- **Distribution start**: Price makes marginal new highs on declining volume. Whipsaws increase. Range expands but net progress slows.
- **Markdown start**: Price breaks below the distribution range. Previous support becomes resistance on retest.

## 2. Supply Dynamics Timing

### Bitcoin Halving Effects
- **Pre-halving (6 months before)**: Historically, price begins trending up as narrative builds. The move is front-run.
- **Halving event**: Often a "sell the news" dip of 5-15%. Do not panic.
- **Post-halving (6-12 months)**: The actual supply shock manifests. This is where the major bull run typically begins. Reduced miner sell pressure compounds over months.
- **Rule**: Do not trade the halving event itself. Trade the 6-12 month post-halving trend. Position before, hold through.

### Token Unlock Effects
- **Immediate impact**: Token unlocks hit price within 24-72 hours. Unlike halvings, there is no lag.
- **Size matters**: Unlocks < 1% of circulating supply = noise. Unlocks 1-5% = moderate pressure. Unlocks > 5% = significant, expect 10-25% downside pressure.
- **Insider behavior**: If insiders have been selling OTC pre-unlock, the actual unlock impact may be muted. Check for unusual OTC volume in the 2 weeks prior.
- **Lock-up expiry clusters**: When multiple investors' locks expire simultaneously, the combined sell pressure is greater than the sum of parts. Coordination failure means they all rush to sell first.

### Staking and Burn Mechanics
- **High staking ratio (>60% of supply staked)**: Effective circulating supply is low. Price is more sensitive to demand changes. Bullish bias.
- **Burn mechanisms**: Deflationary if burn rate > emission rate. Calculate net supply change on a weekly basis. If net supply is declining by > 0.5% annualized, this is a meaningful tailwind.
- **Unstaking queues**: Long unstaking queues (e.g., Ethereum's exit queue) act as a temporary supply lock. Monitor queue length as a leading indicator of sell pressure.

## 3. FDV/MCap Ratio by Market Cap Tier

### Large Caps (Top 20, MCap > $5B)
- **FDV/MCap < 1.2**: Most supply is circulating. Minimal dilution risk. Valuation is "real."
- **FDV/MCap 1.2-2.0**: Moderate future dilution. Acceptable if the project has strong revenue or adoption growth to absorb.
- **FDV/MCap > 2.0**: Rare for large caps. If present, there is a major unlock schedule ahead. Discount the valuation by the FDV/MCap ratio minus 1.

### Mid Caps (Top 21-100, MCap $500M-$5B)
- **FDV/MCap < 1.5**: Healthy. Proceed with standard valuation analysis.
- **FDV/MCap 1.5-3.0**: Caution. Future dilution will suppress returns unless demand growth is exceptional.
- **FDV/MCap > 3.0**: The current market cap is misleading. The "real" valuation is the FDV. Use FDV for all comparisons. Many mid-caps in this range underperform over 12 months.

### Small Caps (MCap < $500M)
- **FDV/MCap < 2.0**: Acceptable for small caps. Some future dilution is normal for growing projects.
- **FDV/MCap 2.0-5.0**: Common in new DeFi/L2 projects. Acceptable ONLY if the team has a credible vesting schedule and the protocol generates meaningful revenue.
- **FDV/MCap > 5.0**: High risk. More than 80% of the supply has not yet entered circulation. These tokens often decline 50-80% as unlocks occur, regardless of project quality.
- **FDV/MCap > 10.0**: Avoid entirely unless there is a specific catalyst that will absorb the incoming supply (major partnership, airdrop-driven adoption).

### Rule
Always compare projects within the same FDV/MCap tier. A project with FDV/MCap of 3 looks cheap next to one with FDV/MCap of 8, but both may be overvalued relative to their circulating supply reality.

## 4. ATH Distance Interpretation

### Context Is Everything

#### In a Confirmed Bull Market
- **10-30% below ATH**: Normal pullback. Buy the dip if fundamentals support it. This is the "easy trade" zone in a bull.
- **30-50% below ATH**: Deeper correction but still within bull market parameters for altcoins (BTC rarely drops >35% in a bull). Opportunity if cycle indicators confirm bull intact.
- **50-70% below ATH**: This is a significant drawdown even for a bull. Verify the bull case is still intact. Check if the project has suffered a fundamental setback (hack, team departure, competitor launch).
- **>70% below ATH**: Even in a bull market, this likely indicates a project-specific problem, not a market-wide dip. Investigate before buying.

#### In a Confirmed Bear Market
- **10-30% below ATH**: The bear is just starting. Expect much more downside. Do NOT buy. This is the "denial" phase.
- **30-50% below ATH**: Bear market territory but potentially only halfway through. Falling knife — do not attempt to catch.
- **50-70% below ATH**: Approaching historical bear market bottoms for large caps. Begin building a watchlist. Do not buy yet unless accumulation signals appear.
- **>70% below ATH**: Historical bottoming zone for quality projects. Start DCA if fundamentals are intact. Accept that it may go lower.
- **>90% below ATH**: Either the project is dead or this is a generational buy. Only buy if the project has active development, growing user base, and a viable path to recovery. Most tokens that drop >90% never recover.

## 5. Institutional Volume Patterns

### Identifying Institutional Activity
- **Block trades on OTC desks**: Large single transactions ($1M+) that do not appear on the order book. Often visible in on-chain data as large wallet-to-wallet transfers.
- **Consistent daily accumulation**: Same-sized buys at regular intervals (TWAP execution). This is algorithmic institutional buying. Bullish.
- **End-of-quarter activity**: Institutions rebalance quarterly. Expect increased volume in the last 2 weeks of each quarter. Price moves during this period may reverse in the first week of the next quarter (window dressing).

### Institutional Entry Signals
- ETF inflow data (for BTC/ETH): Multi-day consecutive inflows > $100M/day = strong institutional bid. This can sustain a rally for weeks.
- Grayscale premium/discount: Premium = institutional demand exceeding supply. Discount = institutional selling or lack of demand.
- CME futures open interest rising: Institutional participation is increasing. This adds legitimacy and liquidity to the trend.

### Institutional Exit Signals
- ETF outflows for 5+ consecutive days.
- CME OI declining while spot OI remains flat = institutions leaving while retail stays.
- Large OTC block sells reported by on-chain analytics.
- Grayscale discount widening.

### Rule
Institutional flows are slow-moving but powerful. They do not cause single-day spikes; they cause multi-week trends. Align with them, do not trade against them.

## 6. When Valuation and Cycle Disagree

### Scenario 1: Fundamentally Cheap + Cycle Says Bear (Markdown)
- **Trust the cycle in the short term** (1-3 months). Cheap assets get cheaper in a bear.
- **Trust the valuation in the medium term** (6-12 months). If fundamentals are genuinely strong, the recovery will be substantial.
- **Action**: Do not buy now. Place alerts at key support levels. Begin DCA only when cycle indicators show accumulation forming.

### Scenario 2: Fundamentally Expensive + Cycle Says Bull (Markup)
- **Trust the cycle in the short term**. Expensive assets get more expensive in a bull. FOMO is a real force.
- **But tighten risk management**. Use trailing stops. Reduce position size compared to a cheap + bull setup.
- **Action**: Ride the trend but with a short leash. The moment cycle indicators show distribution, exit immediately. There will be no second chance at these valuations.

### Scenario 3: Fundamentally Fair + Cycle Is Unclear (Transition)
- **This is a no-trade zone** for fundamental analysis. Neither valuation nor cycle provides an edge.
- **Action**: Defer to technical and sentiment signals. Fundamental analysis adds no value here.

### Hierarchy Rule
- **Within the same cycle phase**: Trust valuation. Cheap outperforms expensive.
- **Across different cycle phases**: Trust the cycle. A cheap asset in markdown will underperform an expensive asset in markup over the near term.
- **At cycle transitions**: Trust neither. Wait for confirmation. Capital preservation > capital deployment.

## 7. Revenue and Usage Metrics

### Protocol Revenue Interpretation
- **Revenue growing > 20% month-over-month**: Strong fundamental signal. This justifies premium valuations.
- **Revenue flat**: Acceptable in a bear. Concerning in a bull (should be growing with market).
- **Revenue declining**: Bearish regardless of price action. Users are leaving or finding alternatives.
- **Revenue per token vs price per token**: If P/Revenue ratio is falling because revenue is growing faster than price, the asset is becoming cheaper in fundamental terms. Bullish.

### Active User Metrics
- **Daily Active Addresses (DAA) growing while price is flat/down**: Bullish divergence. Adoption is happening without price incentive. Strong organic growth signal.
- **DAA declining while price is up**: Bearish divergence. Price is driven by speculation, not usage.
- **DAA spikes (>3x average)**: Often airdrop farming or wash activity. Discount unless sustained for 2+ weeks.

### TVL as a Fundamental Metric
- TVL is a vanity metric for fundamental valuation UNLESS it generates protocol revenue.
- $1B TVL generating $50M annualized revenue > $5B TVL generating $10M annualized revenue.
- Always use Revenue/TVL ratio rather than raw TVL for valuation comparisons.

## 8. Competitive Position Assessment

### Moat Identification
- **Network effects (strong moat)**: More users = more value = more users. Ethereum, major DEXs, dominant L2s.
- **Switching costs (moderate moat)**: Staked capital, integrated tooling, developer ecosystem lock-in.
- **Brand/trust (weak moat in crypto)**: Can evaporate overnight with a hack or scandal. Do not weight heavily.

### Competitive Threat Signals
- A competitor launches with >30% of the incumbent's features and a token incentive program. Expect 10-25% TVL migration within 3 months.
- A major protocol announces a pivot into the project's niche. This is an existential threat for small caps.
- Technology upgrade by a competitor that makes the project's core value proposition obsolete.

### Rule
A fundamentally cheap project losing competitive position is not a bargain — it is a trap. Always check if the "cheapness" is explained by competitive deterioration.

## 9. Synthesis Output Protocol

When producing the final fundamental signal:
1. State the valuation assessment (Cheap, Fair, Expensive) with the primary metric used.
2. State the cycle phase (Accumulation, Markup, Distribution, Markdown) with the evidence.
3. Note any valuation-cycle tension and which to trust in the current context.
4. Flag supply dynamics events in the next 30 days (unlocks, halvings, burns).
5. Report institutional flow direction if available.
6. Produce a single direction (BULLISH, BEARISH, NEUTRAL) with conviction (0.0 to 1.0).
7. Include the time horizon — fundamental signals are inherently longer-term (weeks to months, not hours).
