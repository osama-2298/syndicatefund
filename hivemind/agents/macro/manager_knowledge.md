# Macro Manager Knowledge Base
# Rules for synthesizing crypto-native and external macro signals into actionable output.

---

## 1. Crypto Macro vs External Macro — Lead/Lag Dynamics

### Core Principle
Crypto-native macro signals (BTC dominance, stablecoin flows, crypto-specific regulation) LEAD crypto price action. External macro signals (Fed policy, DXY, equities) LAG in their impact on crypto but set the broader regime.

### Lead Times
- **Crypto-native signals**: 1-7 days lead time. BTC dominance shifts, stablecoin mint/burn events, and exchange reserve changes are near-term predictive.
- **External macro signals**: 1-6 month lag. A rate cut today does not move crypto tomorrow. It changes the capital flow environment over quarters.

### Weighting Rule
- **For trades with a 1-4 week horizon**: Weight crypto-native signals at 60-70%, external macro at 30-40%.
- **For positions with a 1-6 month horizon**: Weight external macro at 50-60%, crypto-native at 40-50%. The macro regime will dominate over time.
- **For cycle-level positioning (6+ months)**: External macro is primary. Crypto does not decouple from global liquidity over full cycles.

## 2. Federal Reserve Policy Transmission

### Rate Cut Transmission to Crypto
- **Announcement day**: Crypto may pump 3-8% on the headline. This is a sentiment trade, not a fundamental one. Often partially reverses within 48 hours.
- **0-1 month post-cut**: Minimal real impact. Capital reallocation has not occurred yet. Noise dominates.
- **1-3 months post-cut**: Risk appetite begins shifting. Capital starts flowing from money markets to risk assets. Crypto benefits but lags equities.
- **3-6 months post-cut**: Full transmission. Lower rates reduce the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like BTC. Institutional allocations adjust. This is where the sustained move begins.
- **Rule**: Do not over-trade rate decisions. Mark the date, project the 3-6 month transmission window, and position accordingly.

### Rate Hike Transmission to Crypto
- Same lag structure as cuts but in reverse.
- Crypto is more sensitive to hikes than cuts because leverage in crypto is higher. Higher rates increase the cost of maintaining leveraged positions, forcing deleverage.
- **Key threshold**: When the Fed Funds Rate exceeds 5%, crypto historically enters a risk-off regime regardless of crypto-native signals.

### Forward Guidance vs Actual Action
- **Hawkish guidance without action**: Markets price in the expectation. Crypto may sell off on guidance alone. But if the actual hike is less than guided, crypto rebounds sharply (relief rally).
- **Dovish guidance without action**: Crypto rallies on hope. But each meeting without a cut erodes the rally. After 3 meetings of "dovish but no cut," the market stops responding.
- **Rule**: Trade the gap between guidance and reality, not the guidance itself.

## 3. BTC Dominance Rotation

### Bear Market / Early Bull (BTC Dominance Rising)
- **What is happening**: Capital is consolidating into BTC as a flight to quality within crypto. Altcoins are underperforming.
- **BTC dominance range**: Typically rises to 55-65% during bear markets.
- **Action**: Overweight BTC. Underweight or avoid altcoins. Even "good" altcoins underperform BTC in this phase.
- **Transition signal**: BTC dominance peaks and begins declining after BTC has rallied 50-100% from the bear market low. This signals the beginning of "alt season."

### Mid Bull (BTC Dominance Falling)
- **What is happening**: Capital is rotating from BTC into altcoins. Risk appetite is expanding. This is "alt season."
- **BTC dominance range**: Falls from 55-65% to 40-50%.
- **Action**: Rotate into fundamentally strong altcoins. L1s and DeFi blue chips first, then mid-caps.
- **Warning**: If BTC dominance falls below 40%, the market is likely in a euphoric phase. Proceed with caution.

### Late Bull / Early Bear (BTC Dominance Volatile)
- **What is happening**: BTC dominance whipsaws as the market transitions. Short-lived alt pumps followed by sharp reversals.
- **Action**: Reduce altcoin exposure. Return to BTC or stablecoins. The risk/reward of altcoins is poor in this phase.
- **Confirmation**: BTC dominance bottoms and begins rising again = bear market is starting. Exit alts immediately.

### BTC Dominance Thresholds
- **>60%**: Bear market or very early bull. BTC only.
- **50-60%**: Transitioning. Selective alts (large caps only).
- **40-50%**: Alt season in progress. Broader alt exposure warranted.
- **<40%**: Euphoria. Begin de-risking the entire portfolio.

## 4. Polymarket Data Interpretation

### High Volume = Reliable Signal
- **Threshold**: A Polymarket prediction market with > $1M in total volume and > 100 unique traders is statistically meaningful.
- **Interpretation**: The market probability reflects aggregated information from participants with skin in the game. Treat it as a calibrated probability estimate.
- **Specific use cases**: Regulatory outcomes (ETF approval probability), election results affecting crypto policy, protocol governance votes.

### Low Volume = Noise
- **Threshold**: Markets with < $100K volume or < 20 unique traders are unreliable.
- **Interpretation**: Too few participants for meaningful price discovery. The odds may reflect a single whale's position, not consensus.
- **Rule**: Do not cite low-volume Polymarket data in synthesis. Mention it only as "unconfirmed market speculation."

### Polymarket Lag and Lead
- **Polymarket leads traditional polling/media by 12-48 hours** for political events (money moves before opinions).
- **Polymarket lags insider information by hours to days** (insiders trade before markets adjust).
- **Rule**: If Polymarket odds shift dramatically (>15 percentage points) in under 24 hours, something material has happened that may not yet be public. Investigate before acting.

### Specific Market Types
- **Binary regulatory outcomes (ETF Yes/No)**: These are the most useful. The probability directly translates to expected value calculations for positioning.
- **Date-based markets (will X happen by Y date)**: Useful but sensitive to time decay. A market at 30% probability with 6 months remaining is different from 30% with 1 week remaining.
- **Multiple outcome markets**: Less useful for crypto macro. The winner-take-all nature makes partial information less actionable.

## 5. Risk-On / Risk-Off Regime Identification

### Risk-On Regime Indicators
- VIX below 18 (equities) — stable, risk-seeking environment.
- DXY declining — dollar weakening, capital flowing to risk assets.
- US 10-year yield declining or stable — borrowing costs favorable.
- High-yield credit spreads narrowing — credit markets healthy.
- BTC correlating positively with NASDAQ — crypto trading as a tech/risk asset.
- Stablecoin market cap growing — new capital entering crypto ecosystem.

### Risk-Off Regime Indicators
- VIX above 25 — elevated fear, capital fleeing risk.
- DXY strengthening — flight to dollar safety.
- US 10-year yield spiking — tightening financial conditions.
- High-yield credit spreads widening — credit stress emerging.
- BTC correlating with gold — crypto trading as a safe haven (rare but meaningful).
- Stablecoin market cap declining — capital leaving crypto ecosystem.

### Regime Change Detection
- **Transition takes 2-4 weeks**. Single-day moves do not constitute a regime change.
- **Confirmation rule**: At least 3 of 6 indicators must flip before declaring a regime change.
- **Crypto sensitivity**: Crypto amplifies regime changes. A risk-off move that causes a 5% equity decline will cause a 10-20% crypto decline.

### Trading Rules by Regime
- **Risk-on**: Full allocation. Favor higher-beta altcoins. Leverage acceptable (moderated).
- **Risk-off**: Reduce to 30-50% allocation. BTC and stablecoins only. No leverage.
- **Transition (ambiguous)**: 50-70% allocation. BTC dominant. No new altcoin positions.

## 6. When Crypto Macro and External Macro Diverge

### Scenario 1: Crypto Bullish, External Bearish
- **Example**: BTC dominance falling, stablecoin inflows rising, BUT Fed is hiking and DXY is strengthening.
- **Interpretation**: Crypto is running on its own momentum (likely a narrative-driven rally or cycle-specific event like a halving). This can work for 1-3 months but is unsustainable.
- **Action**: Trade the crypto momentum but with reduced position sizes and tight stops. The external macro will eventually win.
- **Historical precedent**: Q1 2023 — crypto rallied 50%+ while macro was still tightening. The rally was real but capped.

### Scenario 2: Crypto Bearish, External Bullish
- **Example**: Exchange reserves rising, funding rates negative, BUT Fed is cutting and equities are at ATH.
- **Interpretation**: Crypto-specific issue (hack, regulation, major bankruptcy) is overriding favorable macro. This creates a buying opportunity IF the crypto-specific issue is temporary.
- **Action**: Investigate the crypto-specific bearish driver. If it is structural (fundamental protocol failure, regulatory ban), respect it regardless of macro. If it is transient (FUD, temporary liquidity crunch), start accumulating.
- **Historical precedent**: Post-FTX collapse — macro turned favorable before crypto recovered. The 2-month lag was the buying opportunity.

### Scenario 3: Both Ambiguous
- **Action**: Do not force a directional call. Reduce exposure to market-neutral levels. Wait for clarity.
- **Rule**: Ambiguity is not a signal. It is the absence of a signal. Do not fabricate conviction.

## 7. M2 Money Supply Correlation

### The Relationship
- Global M2 money supply is the strongest long-term macro correlator with BTC price (correlation ~0.85 over 5+ year periods).
- M2 growth leads BTC price by approximately 10-12 weeks (2.5-3 months).
- **Rule**: When M2 is expanding, the macro backdrop for crypto is favorable. When M2 is contracting, the macro backdrop is hostile.

### Actionable Thresholds
- **M2 YoY growth > 6%**: Strongly favorable for crypto. This is the "liquidity flood" environment where all risk assets benefit.
- **M2 YoY growth 2-6%**: Moderately favorable. Crypto can rally but requires additional catalysts (halving, ETF, adoption milestone).
- **M2 YoY growth 0-2%**: Neutral to negative. Crypto may range or slowly decline. No strong directional bias from macro liquidity.
- **M2 YoY growth < 0% (contraction)**: Hostile. Crypto is unlikely to sustain a rally. Bear market conditions unless crypto-specific catalysts overwhelm the headwind.

### Tracking M2
- Use GLOBAL M2, not US-only. China, EU, and Japan monetary policy matters as much as the Fed for global liquidity.
- M2 data is released monthly with a 2-week lag. This is fine — the signal operates on a 3-month lead time anyway.
- **Leading indicator for M2**: Central bank balance sheet changes are published weekly and lead M2 changes by 4-6 weeks. Use these as an early warning system.

## 8. DXY Inverse Correlation

### The Relationship
- The US Dollar Index (DXY) has an inverse correlation with BTC of approximately -0.6 to -0.8 during risk-on regimes.
- The correlation weakens during crypto-specific events (halvings, major hacks) when crypto decouples temporarily.

### DXY Thresholds and Crypto Implications
- **DXY > 105**: Strong dollar. Headwind for crypto. Expect underperformance relative to trend.
- **DXY 100-105**: Moderate dollar strength. Neutral for crypto. Other signals dominate.
- **DXY 95-100**: Weak dollar. Tailwind for crypto. Risk assets broadly favored.
- **DXY < 95**: Very weak dollar. Strongly favorable for crypto. Historical bull runs have coincided with DXY below 95.

### DXY Rate of Change Matters More Than Level
- A DXY dropping from 107 to 103 (falling) is MORE bullish for crypto than a stable DXY at 98.
- A DXY rising from 95 to 100 (rising) is MORE bearish for crypto than a stable DXY at 103.
- **Rule**: Trade the DXY trend direction, not the absolute level. A rapidly weakening dollar is the strongest macro tailwind for crypto.

### DXY Breakout/Breakdown Signals
- DXY breaking above a multi-month resistance: Bearish for crypto. Reduce exposure within 1-2 weeks.
- DXY breaking below a multi-month support: Bullish for crypto. Increase exposure as the breakdown confirms.
- **Timeframe**: DXY trends last 3-12 months typically. Once a DXY trend establishes, it is a persistent macro input.

## 9. Geopolitical and Regulatory Regime

### Regulatory Framework
- **Favorable regulation (clear framework, ETF approvals)**: Reduces uncertainty premium. Bullish over 3-6 months as institutional participation increases.
- **Hostile regulation (bans, enforcement actions)**: Creates immediate sell pressure (1-7 days) but impact duration depends on jurisdiction. US/EU/China actions matter. Small-country bans are noise.
- **Ambiguous regulation (proposed bills, SEC lawsuits without resolution)**: Creates a volatility drag. Markets hate uncertainty more than bad news.

### Geopolitical Risk Transmission
- **War/conflict escalation**: Initially risk-off (sell crypto). But if the conflict threatens the dollar system (sanctions, SWIFT exclusions), BTC may decouple and rally as an alternative rail.
- **Banking crisis**: Short-term risk-off, then bullish for BTC specifically (not alts) as the "bank alternative" narrative activates. See March 2023 SVB crisis for template.
- **Election cycles**: Crypto policy becomes a campaign issue. Polymarket data is the best source. Position AFTER the outcome, not on speculation.

## 10. Synthesis Output Protocol

When producing the final macro signal:
1. State the external macro regime (Risk-On, Risk-Off, Transitioning).
2. State the crypto-native macro condition (Capital Inflow, Capital Outflow, Neutral).
3. Identify any divergence between external and crypto macro, and state which to trust given the time horizon.
4. Report key external metrics: DXY trend, M2 growth rate, Fed policy trajectory.
5. Report key crypto metrics: BTC dominance trend, stablecoin supply trend.
6. Include any Polymarket-derived probabilities for near-term catalysts.
7. Produce a single direction (BULLISH, BEARISH, NEUTRAL) with conviction (0.0 to 1.0).
8. State the time horizon over which this macro signal is valid (typically weeks to months).
